## 01. Information Systems Overview | 信息系统概述  <!-- ref: 01.Introduction.pdf -->

### Information Technology and Information Systems | 信息技术与信息系统  <!-- p=12 -->

[en]
**Information Technology (IT)**: all the hardware and software that a firm needs to use in order to achieve its business objectives. IT generally consists of computing technology, networking (communication) technology, and data management and analytics technology.

**Information System (IS)**: a set of interrelated components that collect, process, store, and distribute information to support decision making, coordination, and control. An IS helps managers and workers analyze problems, visualize complex subjects, and create new products.

**Data vs. Information**:
- `Data` are streams of raw facts.
- `Information` is data shaped into meaningful form.

**Functions of an Information System**:
An information system performs three basic activities—input, processing, and output—to produce the information organizations need. Feedback is output returned to appropriate people or activities in the organization to evaluate and refine input.

**Management Information System (MIS)**:
- Traditionally: IS that support middle-level management.
- Generally: Systems built with IT that facilitate management excellence (i.e., MIS = IS).
- Also refers to the disciplinary field that studies the construction, use, and impacts of information systems.
[/en]

[zh]
**信息技术（IT）**：企业为实现业务目标所需的所有硬件和软件。IT 通常包括计算技术、网络（通信）技术以及数据管理与分析技术。

**信息系统（IS）**：一组相互关联的组件，用于收集、处理、存储和分发信息，以支持决策、协调和控制。IS 帮助管理者和员工分析问题、可视化复杂主题并创造新产品。

**数据与信息**：
- `数据` 是原始事实的流。
- `信息` 是被赋予意义形态的数据。

**信息系统的功能**：
信息系统执行三项基本活动——输入、处理和输出——以产生组织所需的信息。反馈是返回给组织中适当人员或活动的输出，用于评估和优化输入。

**管理信息系统（MIS）**：
- 传统定义：支持组织中层管理的信息系统。
- 一般定义：用信息技术构建、促进卓越管理的系统（即 MIS = IS）。
- 也指研究信息系统的构建、使用和影响的学科领域。
[/en]

### Dimensions of Information Systems | 信息系统的维度  <!-- p=20 -->

[en]
An information system creates value for the firm as an organizational and management solution to challenges posed by the environment. Using IS effectively requires understanding three dimensions:

**1. Organization**:
Includes people, structure, business processes, politics, and culture. Organizations have a hierarchy of authority: senior management → middle management → operational management → knowledge workers → data workers → production/service workers. Business functions include sales and marketing, human resources, finance and accounting, and manufacturing and production.

**2. Management**:
Managers set organizational strategy for responding to business challenges and act creatively to create new products/services or occasionally re-create the organization.

**3. Technology**:
Includes computer hardware and software, data management and analytics technology, and networking/telecommunications technology. IT infrastructure provides the platform that the system is built on.
[/en]

[zh]
信息系统作为应对环境挑战的组织和管理解决方案，为企业创造价值。有效使用 IS 需要理解三个维度：

**1. 组织**：
包括人员、结构、业务流程、政治和文化。组织拥有权威层级：高层管理 → 中层管理 → 运营管理 → 知识工作者 → 数据工作者 → 生产/服务工作者。业务职能包括销售与市场、人力资源、财务与会计、制造与生产。

**2. 管理**：
管理者制定应对业务挑战的组织战略，并创造性地采取行动来创造新产品/服务，或偶尔重塑组织。

**3. 技术**：
包括计算机硬件和软件、数据管理与分析技术、网络/电信技术。IT 基础设施提供了系统构建的平台。
[/en]

### Information Systems, Organizations, and Strategy | 信息系统、组织与战略  <!-- p=36 -->

[en]
**What is an Organization?**
- **Technical definition**: A formal social structure that processes resources from the environment to produce outputs; a formal legal entity with internal rules and procedures, as well as a social structure.
- **Behavioral definition**: A collection of rights, privileges, obligations, and responsibilities that is delicately balanced over time through conflict and conflict resolution.

**Features of Organizations**:
- Goals (Strategy)
- Organizational Structure
- Business Processes
- Organizational Politics
- Organizational Culture
- Organizational Environments

**Porter's Competitive Forces Model**:
Five competitive forces shape the fate of a firm:
1. Traditional competitors
2. New market entrants
3. Substitute products and services
4. Customers
5. Suppliers

**Strategies for Dealing with Competitive Forces**:
- **Low-cost leadership**: Produce at lower prices (e.g., Walmart, Xiaomi)
- **Product differentiation**: Enable new products/services, change customer experience (e.g., Google, Apple, DJI)
- **Focus on market niche**: Specialize in a single market niche (e.g., Transsion, Pop Mart)
- **Strengthen customer and supplier intimacy**: Develop strong ties and loyalty, increase switching costs (e.g., Toyota, SHEIN)

**The Business Value Chain Model**:
Views the firm as a series of activities that add value to products or services. Primary activities vs. support activities.
[/en]

[zh]
**什么是组织？**
- **技术定义**：从环境处理资源以产生输出的正式社会结构；具有内部规则和程序以及社会结构的正式法律实体。
- **行为定义**：在一段时间内通过冲突和冲突解决微妙平衡的权利、特权、义务和责任的集合。

**组织特征**：
- 目标（战略）
- 组织结构
- 业务流程
- 组织政治
- 组织文化
- 组织环境

**波特的竞争力量模型**：
五种竞争力量决定了企业的命运：
1. 传统竞争者
2. 新市场进入者
3. 替代产品和服务
4. 客户
5. 供应商

**应对竞争力量的策略**：
- **低成本领导**：以更低价格生产（如沃尔玛、小米）
- **产品差异化**：创造新产品/服务，改变客户体验（如谷歌、苹果、大疆）
- **聚焦市场利基**：专注于单一市场细分（如传音、泡泡玛特）
- **加强客户与供应商亲密性**：建立牢固关系和忠诚度，提高转换成本（如丰田、希音）

**业务价值链模型**：
将企业视为一系列为产品或服务增加价值的活动。主要活动 vs. 支持活动。
[/en]

### Types of Information Systems | 信息系统的类型  <!-- p=54 -->

[en]
**Systems for Different Management Levels**:
| Level | System Type | Description |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Senior Management | Executive Support Systems (ESS) | Address nonroutine decisions requiring judgment, evaluation, and insight |
| Middle Management | Management Information Systems (MIS) + Decision Support Systems (DSS) | MIS provides reports on current performance; DSS supports nonroutine decision making |
| Operational Management | Transaction Processing Systems (TPS) | Perform and record daily routine transactions |

**Enterprise Applications**:
Systems for linking the enterprise that span functional areas, execute business processes across the firm, and include all levels of management. Four major applications:
- Enterprise Systems (ERP)
- Supply Chain Management Systems (SCM)
- Customer Relationship Management Systems (CRM)
- Knowledge Management Systems (KMS)

**E-business, E-commerce, and E-government**:
- **E-business**: Use of digital technology and Internet to drive major business processes.
- **E-commerce**: Subset of e-business; buying and selling goods and services through the Internet.
- **E-government**: Using Internet technology to deliver information and services to citizens, employees, and businesses.
[/en]

[zh]
**不同管理层级的系统**：
| 层级 | 系统类型 | 描述 |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 高层管理 | 主管支持系统（ESS） | 处理需要判断、评估和洞察的非例行决策 |
| 中层管理 | 管理信息系统（MIS）+ 决策支持系统（DSS） | MIS 提供当前绩效报告；DSS 支持非例行决策 |
| 运营管理 | 事务处理系统（TPS） | 执行和记录日常例行事务 |

**企业应用系统**：
连接企业的系统，跨越职能领域，跨整个公司执行业务流程，包括所有管理层级。四大主要应用：
- 企业系统（ERP）
- 供应链管理系统（SCM）
- 客户关系管理系统（CRM）
- 知识管理系统（KMS）

**电子商务、电子商贸与电子政务**：
- **电子商务（广义）**：使用数字技术和互联网驱动主要业务流程。
- **电子商贸（狭义）**：电子商务的子集；通过互联网买卖商品和服务。
- **电子政务**：使用互联网技术向公民、员工和企业提供信息和服务。
[/en]

### Data, Analytics, and Intelligence | 数据、分析与智能  <!-- p=14 -->

[en]
**Database Management Systems (DBMS)**:
Software that permits an organization to centralize data, manage them efficiently, and provide access to stored data by application programs. A database is a collection of data organized to serve many applications efficiently by centralizing data and controlling redundant data.

**Emerging Database Forms**:
- **NoSQL (Non-relational databases)**: More flexible data model; data sets stored across distributed machines; easier to scale; handles large volumes of unstructured and structured data.
- **Vector Databases**: Stores unstructured data (text, images, audio) as high-dimensional numerical arrays (embeddings) generated by neural networks. Executes semantic similarity search to find data with similar meaning. Functions as the critical "long-term memory" infrastructure for Generative AI, fundamentally powering Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG).
- **Databases in the Cloud**: Examples include Aliyun RDS, Amazon AWS Database, Microsoft SQL Azure Database. Benefits: better response to real-time demand, low costs.

**Information Utilization Levels**:
- Data Warehouse: Stores current and historical data from many core operational transaction systems. Provides analysis and reporting tools.
- Analytical Tools:
  - Multidimensional data analysis (OLAP - Online Analytical Processing)
  - Data mining (Associations, Sequences, Classification, Clustering, Forecasting)
  - Text mining and Web mining

**Large Language Models (LLM) in Business Contexts**:
- **Prompt Engineering**: Connecting to general LLM services with well-designed prompts.
- **Fine-tuning**: Customizing the LLM for a specific business context.
- **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)**: Combining local knowledge with LLM services for contextualized information.
- **Contextualized AI Agents**: For automatic services.
[/en]

[zh]
**数据库管理系统（DBMS）**：
允许组织集中数据、高效管理数据并为应用程序提供对存储数据访问的软件。数据库是通过集中数据和控制冗余数据来高效服务多个应用程序的数据集合。

**新兴数据库形式**：
- **NoSQL（非关系型数据库）**：更灵活的数据模型；数据集存储在分布式机器上；更易扩展；处理大量非结构化和结构化数据。
- **向量数据库**：将非结构化数据（文本、图像、音频）存储为神经网络生成的高维数值数组（嵌入向量）。执行语义相似性搜索以查找含义相似的数据。作为生成式 AI 的关键"长期记忆"基础设施，从根本上支持检索增强生成（RAG）。
- **云端数据库**：例如阿里云 RDS、亚马逊 AWS 数据库、微软 SQL Azure 数据库。优势：更好响应实时需求，保持低成本。

**信息利用层级**：
- 数据仓库：存储来自多个核心运营事务系统的当前和历史数据。提供分析和报告工具。
- 分析工具：
  - 多维数据分析（OLAP - 联机分析处理）
  - 数据挖掘（关联、序列、分类、聚类、预测）
  - 文本挖掘和 Web 挖掘

**业务环境中的大语言模型（LLM）**：
- **提示词工程**：通过精心设计的提示连接到通用 LLM 服务。
- **微调**：为特定业务环境定制 LLM。
- **检索增强生成（RAG）**：结合本地知识与 LLM 服务以获取情境化信息。
- **情境化 AI 智能体**：用于自动服务。
[/en]

### Security and Availability | 安全与可用性  <!-- p=41 -->

[en]

**Availability**: Policies, procedures, and technical measures used to ensure system services and data resources are always accessible to authorized users.

- Software Vulnerability

**Tools and Technologies for Security**:
- Identity Management and Authentication (Passwords, Tokens, Smart cards, Biometric authentication, Two-factor authentication)
- Firewalls
- Intrusion Detection Systems
- Antivirus Software
- Encryption (Symmetric key, Asymmetric/Public key)
- Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) and Digital Certificates
- High-availability Systems: Redundancy and Backup

**Organizational Framework for Security and Control**:
- Information Systems Controls (General controls, Application controls)
- Security Policy and Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)
- Auditing and Risk Assessment
- Disaster Recovery Planning and Business Continuity Planning
[/en]

### Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) | 企业资源计划  <!-- p=6 -->

[en]
**Evolution of Manufacturing System Management**:
- **ROP (Reorder Point)**: Early inventory management method (1940s).
- **MRP (Material Requirement Planning)**: Production planning, scheduling, and inventory control system to manage manufacturing processes. Solves: minimal completion time, minimal cost, maximal production using Bill of Materials (BOM).
- **MRP II (Manufacturing Resource Planning)**: Method for effective planning of all resources directly related to production (Money, Manpower, Materials, production capacity). Adds "what-if" simulation.
- **ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)**: Business management software/system, usually a suite of integrated applications that collect, store, manage, and interpret data from many business activities including product planning, manufacturing, marketing and sales, inventory management, shipping and payment.

**Key Features of ERP**:
- Suite of integrated software modules and a common central database.
- Collects data from many divisions of the firm for use in nearly all internal business activities.
- Information entered in one process is immediately available for other processes.
- Integration: Functional areas are integrated.
- Centralization: Databases are centralized.

**Major ERP Vendors**: SAP, Oracle (PeopleSoft), Microsoft, Sage, Epicor, Infor, Odoo (open source), Yonyou (用友), Kingdee (金蝶).

**Implementation of ERP**:
- Built around thousands of predefined business processes reflecting best practices.
- To implement, firms: select functions, conduct Business Process Re-engineering (BPR), map business processes to software processes, use configuration tables for customizing.

**Risks of ERP**:
- Conflicts with business strategy ("best practices" may not be best for you)
- Huge investments
- Conflicts with organizational culture and politics (organizational resistance)
- Changes in employee responsibilities
- Loss of flexibility

**Key Success Factors for Implementing ERP**:
- Organization is prepared for change.
- Initiative is seen as business imperative.
- Executive leaders are active and visible in support.
- Resources are available.
- Good packaged system is used and customized.
- Users are trained before implementation.
- Stress the enterprise, not the system.
- General manager acts as mediator.
[/en]

[zh]
**制造管理系统演进**：
- **ROP（订货点法）**：早期库存管理方法（1940年代）。
- **MRP（物料需求计划）**：用于管理制造过程的生产计划、调度和库存控制系统。解决：最小完成时间、最小成本、最大产量，使用物料清单（BOM）。
- **MRP II（制造资源计划）**：有效规划所有与生产直接相关的资源（资金、人力、材料、生产能力）的方法。增加"假设"模拟。
- **ERP（企业资源计划）**：企业管理软件/系统，通常是一套集成应用，从许多业务活动中收集、存储、管理和解释数据，包括产品规划、制造、营销与销售、库存管理、发货与支付。

**ERP 的关键特征**：
- 集成软件模块套件和公共中央数据库。
- 从公司多个部门收集数据，供几乎所有内部业务活动使用。
- 在一个过程中输入的信息立即可用于其他过程。
- 集成：职能领域被整合。
- 集中化：数据库被集中。

**主要 ERP 供应商**：SAP、Oracle（PeopleSoft）、Microsoft、Sage、Epicor、Infor、Odoo（开源）、用友、金蝶。

**ERP 的实施**：
- 围绕成千上万个反映最佳实践的预定义业务流程构建。
- 实施步骤：选择功能、进行业务流程重组（BPR）、将业务流程映射到软件流程、使用配置表进行定制。

**ERP 的风险**：
- 与业务战略冲突（"最佳实践"可能并非最适合你）
- 巨额投资
- 与组织文化和政治冲突（组织抵制）
- 员工职责变化
- 失去灵活性

**ERP 实施的关键成功因素**：
- 组织为变革做好准备。
- 倡议被视为业务要务。
- 高管领导积极且明显地支持。
- 资源可用。
- 使用和定制了良好的套件系统。
- 实施前对用户进行培训。
- 强调企业而非系统。
- 总经理充当调解人。
[/en]

### Supply Chain Management (SCM) | 供应链管理  <!-- p=31 -->

[en]
**Supply Chain**: Network of organizations and processes for procuring materials, transforming them into products, and distributing the products.
- Upstream supply chain: Firm's suppliers, suppliers' suppliers.
- Downstream supply chain: Organizations and processes for delivering products to customers.
- Internal supply chain.

**Bullwhip Effect (牛鞭效应)**: Information about product demand gets distorted as it passes from one entity to the next across the supply chain. Causes: uncertain customer orders, replenishment delays, order batching, price fluctuations, rationing and shortage gaming.

**Vendor Managed Inventory (VMI)**: Suppliers manage inventory levels for customers (e.g., P&G and Walmart). Benefits: reduced inventory costs, improved efficiency.

**Push Strategy vs. Pull Strategy**:
- Push ("sell what we make"): Work release based on downstream demand forecasts. Proactive, but forecast uncertainty.
- Pull ("make what we sell"): Work release based on actual demand. Reactive, but delayed information.

**Push-Pull Strategy**: Hybrid approach. Early stages use push based on aggregated forecasts; final production uses pull based on specific customer demand.

**Supply Chain Management Systems**:
- Supply Chain Planning Systems: Model existing supply chain, enable demand planning, optimize sourcing/manufacturing plans, establish inventory levels, identify transportation modes.
- Supply Chain Execution Systems: Manage flow of products through distribution centers and warehouses.
[/en]

[zh]
**供应链**：采购材料、将其转化为产品并分销产品的组织和流程网络。
- 上游供应链：企业的供应商、供应商的供应商。
- 下游供应链：负责将产品交付给客户的组织和流程。
- 内部供应链。

**牛鞭效应**：产品需求信息在供应链中从一个实体传递到下一个实体时被扭曲。原因：客户订单不确定、补货延迟、批量订购、价格波动、配给与短缺博弈。

**供应商管理库存（VMI）**：供应商为客户管理库存水平（如宝洁与沃尔玛）。收益：降低库存成本，提高效率。

**推式策略 vs. 拉式策略**：
- 推式（"生产什么卖什么"）：基于下游需求预测发布工作。主动，但预测存在不确定性。
- 拉式（"卖什么生产什么"）：基于实际需求发布工作。被动，但信息存在延迟。

**推-拉混合策略**：混合方法。早期阶段使用基于汇总预测的推式；最终生产使用基于特定客户需求的拉式。

**供应链管理系统**：
- 供应链计划系统：建模现有供应链，实现需求计划，优化采购/制造计划，设定库存水平，确定运输模式。
- 供应链执行系统：管理产品通过配送中心和仓库的流动。
[/en]

### Knowledge Management | 知识管理  <!-- p=27 -->

[en]
**Uses of Knowledge Management**:
1. Employees find answers to questions.
2. Train employees on new processes.
3. Speed up time-to-productivity and time-to-value.
4. Collaborate (e.g., cross-functional report writing).
5. Tap into expert knowledge.
6. Easily share and find information.

**Explicit vs. Tacit Knowledge**:
- **Explicit Knowledge**: Knowledge that has been documented. Structured (enterprise applications) or semi-structured/unstructured (main content of KMS).

**Major Types of Knowledge Management Systems**:
- Enterprise-wide Knowledge Management Systems: Enterprise Content Management (ECM), collaboration and social tools, learning management systems.

**LLMs in Knowledge Management**:

**Challenges of LLMs in Knowledge Management**:
- Hallucination: Generating plausible but factually incorrect information.
- Data Privacy and Security: Risk of exposing sensitive corporate data.
- Dynamic Access Control: RAG and AI agents must respect user role boundaries.
- Cost and Maintenance: High computational costs, overhead of maintaining vector databases.

## 06. Systems Analysis Fundamentals | 系统分析基础  <!-- ref: 06.Systems_Analysis (1).pdf -->

### The Need for Systems Analysis and Design | 系统分析与设计的必要性  <!-- p=6 -->

[en]
**Systems analysis and design** is the process to analyze, design, and implement improvements through the use of computerized information systems in support of users and the functioning of businesses.

Why is it needed?
- To align systems (solutions) with business targets.
- To structure and guide systems development, which is a costly endeavor.
- To involve users.

**Failure Case Examples**:
- **Lidl's €500 Million SAP Disaster (2018)**: Inadequate requirements understanding (inventory at purchase prices vs. retail prices); refusal to re-engineer business processes; massive customizations that made the system slow and unstable.
- **Moutai's Data Middle Platform Collapse (2020)**: Ignored organizational culture (agile platform vs. rigid SOE); over-centralization; excluded line-of-business managers from requirements gathering.
- **MissFresh Supply-Chain Algorithm Bankruptcy (2022)**: Underestimated demand volatility and spoilage risks; no margin-aware cost modeling; prioritized technical feasibility over economic feasibility.
[/en]

[zh]
**系统分析与设计**是通过使用计算机化信息系统来分析、设计和实施改进以支持用户和企业运营的过程。

为什么需要？
- 使系统（解决方案）与业务目标对齐。
- 构建和指导系统开发，这是一项成本高昂的工作。
- 让用户参与。

**失败案例**：
- **Lidl 的 5 亿欧元 SAP 灾难（2018）**：需求理解不足（按采购价 vs. 零售价计算库存）；拒绝重新设计业务流程；大量定制导致系统缓慢和不稳定。
- **茅台数据中台崩溃（2020）**：忽视组织文化（敏捷平台 vs. 僵化的国企）；过度集中化；排除了业务部门管理者的需求收集。
- **每日优鲜供应链算法破产（2022）**：低估需求波动和损耗风险；没有考虑利润率的成本建模；技术可行性优先于经济可行性。

### Development Methodologies | 开发方法论  <!-- p=19 -->

[en]
**Three Major Development Methodologies**:

### Understanding and Modeling Organizational Systems | 理解与建模组织系统  <!-- p=37 -->

[en]
**Three Modeling Techniques**:
1. **Context-Level Data Flow Diagrams (DFD)**: Dynamic modeling. Shows scope of system: what is included (processes) and what is external (entities). One process (0), external entities, major data flows.
2. **Entity-Relationship Model (ERD)**: Static modeling. Shows entities and their relationships (one-to-one, one-to-many, many-to-many).
3. **Use Case Modeling (UML)**: Functional modeling. Describes what the system does without describing how. Actors (external users/roles), use cases (tasks), relationships (communicates, includes, extends, generalizes). Accompanied by use case scenarios.

**Use Case Relationships**:
- **Communicates**: Actor connects to use case.
- **Include (<<include>>)**: A use case contains behavior common to multiple use cases.
- **Extend (<<extend>>)**: A use case handles exceptions from the basic use case.
- **Generalizes**: One "thing" is more general than another.
[/en]

[zh]
**三种建模技术**：
1. **上下文级数据流图（DFD）**：动态建模。显示系统范围：包含的内容（过程）和外部内容（实体）。一个过程（0）、外部实体、主要数据流。
2. **实体-关系模型（ERD）**：静态建模。显示实体及其关系（一对一、一对多、多对多）。
3. **用例建模（UML）**：功能建模。描述系统做什么而不描述如何做。参与者（外部用户/角色）、用例（任务）、关系（通讯、包含、扩展、泛化）。伴随有用例场景。

**用例关系**：
- **通讯**：参与者连接到用例。
- **包含（<<include>>）**：一个用例包含多个用例共有的行为。
- **扩展（<<extend>>）**：一个用例处理基本用例的异常。
- **泛化**：一个"事物"比另一个更一般化。
[/en]

### Interactive Methods | 交互式方法  <!-- p=6 -->

[en]
**Three Interactive Methods to Elicit Human Information Requirements**:
1. **Interviewing**: Important for collecting data on human and system requirements. Reveals opinions, feelings, goals, HCI concerns.
   - Question types: Open-ended, Closed, Bipolar, Probes
   - Question structures: Pyramid (closed → open), Funnel (open → closed), Diamond (closed-open-closed)
   - Preparation steps: Read background material, set objectives, decide whom to interview, prepare interviewee, decide question types/structure.

2. **Joint Application Design (JAD)**: Replaces series of interviews with group sessions. Saves time, rapid development, improved user ownership. Requires large block of time, good preparation, conducive culture.
3. **Questionnaires**: Reach many stakeholders efficiently, quantify opinions, prioritize features. Open-ended, closed/scale, ranking questions. Avoid bias, ensure reliability/validity.

**Outcome of Information Requirement Analysis**: Requirements-Specification Document (需求说明书) containing: project overview, stakeholder analysis, functional requirements (prioritized use cases), non-functional requirements, business rules, data requirements, assumptions & constraints.

[zh]
**三种获取人类信息需求的交互式方法**：

### Data Flow Diagrams | 数据流图  <!-- p=18 -->

[en]
**Data Flow Diagram (DFD)**: Graphically characterizes data processes and flows in a business system. Depicts system inputs, processes, outputs.

**Basic Symbols**:
- Process (rounded rectangle)
- Data flow (arrow)
- Data store (open-ended rectangle)
- External entity (square or rectangle)

**Basic Rules**:
- Must have at least one process.
- No freestanding objects.
- Process must have both input and output data flow.
- Data store must connect to at least one process.
- External entities should not connect to each other.

**Developing DFDs – Top-Down Approach**:
1. Create Context Diagram (highest level; one process numbered 0; all external entities and major data flows)
2. Draw Diagram 0 (next level; up to 9 processes; major data stores)
3. Create Child Diagrams (explode each process; lower level number = parent process number; primitive process = not exploded)
4. Check for errors
5. Develop physical DFD from logical DFD
6. Partition the physical DFD

**Logical vs. Physical DFD**:
- Logical: how business operates (business activities)
- Physical: how system will be implemented (programs, modules, manual procedures, physical files)

**Event Modeling**: Input flow from external entity is a trigger that starts process activities. Event response table maps events to triggers, activities, responses, destinations.

**Partitioning**: Divide DFD into collections of manual procedures and computer programs based on: different user groups, timing, similar tasks, efficiency, consistency of data, security.
[/en]

[zh]
**数据流图（DFD）**：图形化表征业务系统中的数据过程和流。描绘系统输入、过程、输出。

**基本符号**：
- 过程（圆角矩形）
- 数据流（箭头）
- 数据存储（开口矩形）
- 外部实体（正方形或矩形）

**基本规则**：
- 必须至少有一个过程。
- 不能有独立对象。
- 过程必须有输入和输出数据流。
- 数据存储必须连接到至少一个过程。
- 外部实体不应相互连接。

**开发 DFD——自顶向下方法**：
1. 创建上下文图（最高层；一个过程编号为0；所有外部实体和主要数据流）
2. 绘制图0（下一层；最多9个过程；主要数据存储）
3. 创建子图（展开每个过程；子图编号 = 父过程编号；原语过程 = 未展开）
4. 检查错误
5. 从逻辑 DFD 开发物理 DFD
6. 划分物理 DFD

**逻辑 DFD 与物理 DFD**：
- 逻辑：业务如何运作（业务活动）
- 物理：系统将如何实施（程序、模块、手工流程、物理文件）

**事件建模**：来自外部实体的输入流是启动过程活动的触发器。事件响应表将事件映射到触发器、活动、响应、目的地。

**划分**：根据不同用户组、时机、类似任务、效率、数据一致性、安全性，将 DFD 划分为手工流程和计算机程序的集合。

### Data Dictionaries | 数据字典  <!-- p=4 -->

[en]

### Process Specifications and Structured Decisions | 过程规范与结构化决策  <!-- p=18 -->

[en]
**Process Specifications (Minispecs)**: Created for primitive processes. Goals: reduce ambiguity, obtain precise description, validate system design. Contents: process number/name, description, input/output data flows, process type, prewritten code, logic description, unresolved issues.

**Three Techniques for Structured Decision Logic**:

1. **Structured English**: Used when logic involves formulas or iteration, and decisions are not complex.
   - Structures: Sequential (action #1, #2, #3), Decision (IF-THEN-ELSE), Case (IF-CASE), Iteration (DO WHILE).
   - Advantages: Clarifies logic, effective communication with users.

2. **Decision Tables**: Used for complex combinations of conditions, actions, and rules. Four quadrants: conditions, condition alternatives, actions, rules.
   - Helps detect incompleteness, impossible situations, contradictions, redundancy.
   - Can simplify by combining rules.

3. **Decision Trees**: Used when complex branching occurs or sequence of decisions is critical. Draw from left to right, list all alternatives.
   - Advantages: Order of checking conditions is immediately noticeable; more readily understood.

**Selection Guide**:
- Structured English: Many repetitious actions, communication important.
- Decision Tables: Complex combinations, need to avoid errors.
- Decision Trees: Sequence critical, not every condition relevant to every action.
[/en]

[zh]
**过程规范（Mini规范）**：为原语过程创建。目标：减少歧义、获得精确描述、验证系统设计。内容：过程编号/名称、描述、输入/输出数据流、过程类型、预编写代码、逻辑描述、未解决问题。

**结构化决策逻辑的三种技术**：

1. **结构化自然语言**：当逻辑涉及公式或迭代且决策不复杂时使用。
   - 结构：顺序（操作#1、#2、#3）、决策（IF-THEN-ELSE）、分支（IF-CASE）、迭代（DO WHILE）。
   - 优势：阐明逻辑，与用户有效沟通。

2. **决策表**：用于条件、操作和规则的复杂组合。四个象限：条件、条件备选、操作、规则。
   - 帮助发现不完整、不可能情况、矛盾、冗余。
   - 可以通过合并规则来简化。

3. **决策树**：当出现复杂分支或决策顺序至关重要时使用。从左到右绘制，列出所有备选方案。
   - 优势：条件检查顺序一目了然；更易理解。

**选择指南**：
- 结构化自然语言：多个重复操作、沟通重要。
- 决策表：复杂组合、需要避免错误。
- 决策树：顺序关键、并非每个条件都与每个操作相关。
[/en]

## 10. Object-Oriented Systems Analysis and Design | 面向对象的系统分析与设计  <!-- ref: 10.Object_Orient_Analysis.pdf -->

### Object-Oriented Concepts | 面向对象概念  <!-- p=4 -->

[en]
**Object**: Persons, places, or things relevant to the system being analyzed. An object has structure (attributes) and behavior (methods).

**Class**: Defines the set of shared attributes and behaviors found in each object in the class. Instantiate = create an object from a class.

**Inheritance**: When a derived class inherits all the attributes and behaviors of the base class. Reduces programming labor by using common objects easily.

**Object-Oriented Analysis and Design**: Works well for complicated systems undergoing continuous change. Objects and classes are reusable. UML is the industry standard.
[/en]

[zh]
**对象**：与待分析系统相关的人、地点或事物。对象具有结构（属性）和行为（方法）。

**类**：定义类中每个对象共有的属性和行为集。实例化 = 从类创建对象。

**继承**：派生类继承基类的所有属性和行为。通过使用通用对象减少编程工作量。

**面向对象的分析与设计**：适用于正在持续变更的复杂系统。对象和类可复用。UML 是行业标准。
[/en]

### Unified Modeling Language (UML) | 统一建模语言（UML）  <!-- p=11 -->

[en]
**UML Components**:
- Things: Structural (classes, interfaces, use cases), Behavioral (interactions, state machines), Group (boundaries), Annotation (notes).
- Relationships: Structural (dependencies, aggregations, associations, generalizations/specializations); Behavioral (communicates, includes, extends, generalizes).
- Diagrams: Structural (class, object, component, deployment); Behavioral (use case, sequence, communication, statechart, activity).

**UML Diagram Types**:

| Diagram | Purpose |
|:---|:---|
| Use Case Diagram | Describes what the system does (actors, use cases, relationships) |
| Activity Diagram | Shows sequence of activities, parallel activities, decisions; swimlanes |
| Sequence Diagram | Illustrates interactions between classes/objects over time; emphasizes time ordering |
| Communication Diagram | Shows same as sequence but emphasizes organization of objects |
| Class Diagram | Static features; classes, attributes, methods, relationships (associations, whole/part, aggregation, composition) |
| Statechart Diagram | Examines different states an object may have; complex life cycles |

**Types of Classes**:
- Entity classes: Real-world items (like ERD entities)
- Interface/Boundary classes: User interaction (displays, windows, webpages)
- Abstract classes: Cannot be directly instantiated; generalization/specialization
- Control classes: Control flow of activities
[/en]

[zh]
**UML 组成部分**：
- 事物：结构（类、接口、用例）、行为（交互、状态机）、分组（边界）、注释（笔记）。
- 关系：结构（依赖、聚合、关联、泛化/特化）；行为（通讯、包含、扩展、泛化）。
- 图：结构（类图、对象图、组件图、部署图）；行为（用例图、序列图、通信图、状态图、活动图）。

**UML 图类型**：

| 图 | 目的 |
|:---|:---|
| 用例图 | 描述系统做什么（参与者、用例、关系） |
| 活动图 | 显示活动序列、并行活动、决策；泳道 |
| 序列图 | 展示类/对象之间随时间推移的交互；强调时间顺序 |
| 通信图 | 内容与序列图相同但强调对象组织 |
| 类图 | 静态特征；类、属性、方法、关系（关联、整体/部分、聚合、组合） |
| 状态图 | 检查对象可能具有的不同状态；复杂生命周期 |

**类的类型**：
- 实体类：现实世界项目（如 ERD 实体）
- 接口/边界类：用户交互（显示屏、窗口、网页）
- 抽象类：不能直接实例化；泛化/特化
- 控制类：控制活动流程
[/en]

## 12. Designing Databases and Human-Computer Interaction | 数据库与人机交互设计  <!-- ref: 12.Designing_DB_HCI.pdf -->

### Databases: The Memory of the System | 数据库：系统的记忆  <!-- p=6 -->

[en]
**Relational Databases**: Data organized into tables (relations) with rows (tuples/records) and columns (fields/attributes). Primary key uniquely identifies each row. Foreign key links tables.

**Normalization (规范化)**: Process of eliminating data redundancy and anomalies.
| Normal Form | Rule |
|:---|:---|
| 1NF | Atomic values, no repeating groups |
| 2NF | 1NF + no partial dependency on composite key |
| 3NF | 2NF + no transitive dependency |

- Aim for 3NF by default.
- Denormalize intentionally when query performance demands it.

**OLTP vs. OLAP**:
| | OLTP (Operational) | OLAP (Analytical) |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Purpose | Run the business | Understand the business |
| Focus | Consistency | Analyzability |
| Schema | Normalized (2NF-3NF) | Denormalized |

**NoSQL Databases**:
- Key-Value (Redis): Sacrifices query expressiveness for speed
- Document (MongoDB): Flexible nested data without strict schema
- Column-Family (Cassandra): Massive write scale, no joins/transactions
- Graph (Neo4j): Native relationship traversal

**Vector Databases (向量数据库)** : Store unstructured data as high-dimensional numerical arrays (embeddings). Execute semantic similarity search. Critical for RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation).
[/en]

[zh]
**关系型数据库**：数据组织成表（关系），包含行（元组/记录）和列（字段/属性）。主键唯一标识每一行。外键链接表。

**规范化**：消除数据冗余和异常的过程。
| 范式 | 规则 |
|:---|:---|
| 1NF | 原子值，无重复组 |
| 2NF | 1NF + 无对复合键的部分依赖 |
| 3NF | 2NF + 无传递依赖 |

- 默认目标是 3NF。
- 当查询性能需要时有意识地反规范化。

**OLTP vs. OLAP**：
| | OLTP（运营型） | OLAP（分析型） |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 目的 | 运行业务 | 理解业务 |
| 焦点 | 一致性 | 可分析性 |
| 模式 | 规范化（2NF-3NF） | 反规范化 |

**NoSQL 数据库**：
- 键值（Redis）：牺牲查询表达能力换取速度
- 文档（MongoDB）：灵活的嵌套数据，无严格模式
- 列族（Cassandra）：大规模写入扩展，无连接/事务
- 图（Neo4j）：原生关系遍历

**向量数据库**：将非结构化数据存储为高维数值数组（嵌入向量）。执行语义相似性搜索。对 RAG（检索增强生成）至关重要。
[/en]

### Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) | 人机交互  <!-- p=20 -->

[en]
**Core Principle of HCI**: Designing for fit between human, the computer, and the task.

**Types of User Interfaces**:
- Natural-language, Q&A, Menus, Form-fill, Command-language (CLI), Graphical (GUI), Web interfaces. Applications usually combine multiple types.

**Seven Types of User Feedback**:
1. Accepting input ("I heard you")
2. Recognizing task ("I understand what you want")
3. Producing more information ("I need more from you")
4. Accepting/processing ("Working on it...")
5. Rejecting input with reason ("I can't do that because...")
6. Communicating delay ("This is taking a while")
7. Completing task ("Done. Here's the result.")

**The AI Era: Interaction as Conversation**:
- Traditional: "Teach the user to think like the database."
- AI-Native: "Teach the system to think like the user."
- Agentic: "Delegate to the system to act on your behalf."

Agentic interfaces: User expresses a GOAL; the system PLANS and EXECUTES multiple steps autonomously. Requires tools (APIs, code execution), memory across turns, human-in-the-loop checkpoints.
[/en]

[zh]
**人机交互的核心原则**：设计人与计算机和任务之间的适配。

**用户界面类型**：
- 自然语言、问答、菜单、表单填充、命令语言（CLI）、图形界面（GUI）、Web 界面。应用程序通常组合多种类型。

**七种用户反馈类型**：
1. 接受输入（"我听到了"）
2. 识别任务（"我明白你想要什么"）
3. 请求更多信息（"我需要你提供更多信息"）

7. 完成任务（"完成。这是结果。"）

**AI 时代：作为对话的交互**：
- 传统："教用户像数据库一样思考。"
- AI 原生："教系统像用户一样思考。"
- 智能体："将任务委托给系统，让它代表你行动。"

智能体界面：用户表达目标；系统自主规划和执行多步骤行动。需要工具（API、代码执行）、跨轮次记忆、人在回路检查点。

## 13. Quality Assurance | 质量保证  <!-- ref: 13.Quality_Assurance.pdf -->

### Designing Accurate Data Entry Procedures | 设计准确的数据录入流程  <!-- p=4 -->

[en]
**Effective Coding (数据编码)** :
- **Simple Sequence Code**: Assigned number with no relation to data itself. Best for identifiers (e.g., customer_id). Stable, privacy-safe, deletable without breaking references.
- **Block Sequence Code**: Groups data by common characteristics. Useful for events that may be classified (e.g., order_id: [type]-[date]-[sequence]).
- **Significant-Digit Subset Code**: Each digit position has meaning. Useful for products (e.g., SKU). Enables bulk operations (e.g., "all speakers 20% off").
- Other codes: Classification, Cipher, Mnemonic, Function.

**Coding Guidelines**: Concise, stable, unique, sortable, avoid confusion, uniform, modifiable, meaningful.

**Effective Data Capture – The Core Principle**:
- Enter what only humans can know; let the computer figure out the rest.
- A base data item should be entered only once at only one point.

**Validation (Check Digit)**: Eight types of validation:
1. Missing Data
2. Field Length
3. Class/Composition (character type)
4. Range/Reasonableness
5. Invalid values (blacklist)
6. Comparison with Stored Data (foreign key existence)
7. Self-Checking Digits (Luhn algorithm)
8. Pattern Match (regex)

**Two Layers of Defense**:
- Client-Side Validation: Instant feedback, catches honest mistakes.
- Server-Side Validation: Final defense, enforces business rules, protects against malicious input.
[/en]

[zh]
**有效编码**：
- **简单序列码**：分配的编号与数据本身无关。最适合标识符（如 customer_id）。稳定、隐私安全、删除时不会破坏引用。
- **分类序列编码**：按共同特征分组数据。适用于可能需要分类的事件（如 order_id：[类型]-[日期]-[序列]）。
- **子集代号编码**：每个数字位置都有含义。适用于产品（如 SKU）。支持批量操作（如"所有扬声器八折"）。
- 其他代码：分类码、密码、助记码、功能码。

**编码指南**：简洁、稳定、唯一、可排序、避免混淆、统一、可修改、有意义。

**有效数据捕获——核心原则**：
- 只输入人类才能知道的信息；让计算机计算出其余部分。
- 一个基础数据项应只在一个点输入一次。

**验证**：八种验证类型：
1. 缺失数据
2. 字段长度
3. 类别/组成（字符类型）
4. 范围/合理性
5. 无效值（黑名单）
6. 与存储数据比较（外键存在性）
7. 自校验位（Luhn 算法）
8. 模式匹配（正则表达式）

**两道防线**：
- 客户端验证：即时反馈，捕捉诚实错误。
- 服务器端验证：最后防线，执行业务规则，防止恶意输入。
[/en]

### Quality Assurance and Implementation | 质量保证与实施  <!-- p=26 -->

[en]
**Total Quality Management (TQM)** : Full organizational support of quality management; early commitment to quality.

**Test-First Principle**:
- Test plan: All preparations for tests.
- Unit testing: Tests each program separately.
- System testing: Tests functioning of system as a whole.
- Acceptance testing: Ensures system is ready for production.

**Structured Walkthroughs**: Use peer reviewers to monitor development; point out problems; allow changes.

**Taming Complexity: Modularity**:
- Divide and conquer principle.
- Each module does ONE thing.
- Top-down approach: whole → subsystems → modules.

**Documentation**: "If it isn't written down, it doesn't exist." Docs-as-Code (Git, versioned, AI-assisted).

**Conversion Strategies**:
- Parallel: Old and new run together.
- Direct cutover: Old stops, new starts.
- Pilot: New in one location first.
- Phased: New in stages.

**Security, Recovery, and Evaluation**:
- Security: Authentication, authorization, availability, confidentiality.
- Disaster Recovery: Backup and redundancy.
- Evaluation: Define "success" before building.
[/en]

[zh]
**全面质量管理（TQM）**：组织全力支持质量管理；早期承诺质量。

**测试先行原则**：
- 测试计划：测试的所有准备。
- 单元测试：分别测试每个程序。
- 系统测试：测试整个系统的功能。
- 验收测试：确保系统可用于生产环境。

**结构化复核**：使用同行评审者监控开发；指出问题；允许修改。

**控制复杂性：模块化**：
- 分而治之原则。
- 每个模块只做一件事。
- 自顶向下方法：整体 → 子系统 → 模块。

**文档化**："如果没有写下来，它就不存在。" Docs-as-Code（Git、版本化、AI 辅助）。

**切换策略**：
- 并行：新旧系统一起运行。
- 直接切换：旧系统停止，新系统启动。
- 试点：首先在一个地点引入新系统。
- 分阶段：分阶段引入新系统。

### Trends and Open Issues | 趋势与开放问题  <!-- p=7 -->

[en]
**1. Digital Transformation and Ecosystems**:
- Organizations increasingly operate through digital platforms, APIs, cloud services, mobile apps.
- Boundaries become fluid; coordination extends beyond firm; platform governance becomes central.
- Future of MIS is ecosystem coordination.

**2. Algorithmic Decision-Making**:
- MIS moving from recording transactions to analyzing patterns, predicting outcomes, recommending actions, automating decisions.
- Open issues: data quality, bias, explainability, accountability, overreliance, human oversight, responsible governance.

**3. Human-AI Relationship**:
- Automation: AI performs tasks instead of humans.
- Augmentation: AI helps humans perform tasks better.
- Guardianship: Humans raise/cultivate AI agents and take accountability.
- Central design challenge: responsibility design, not just interface design.

**Final Message**:
To analyze and design information systems is to participate in the design of organizational intelligence. Be critical users, thoughtful analysts, and responsible designers of digital futures.
[/en]

[zh]
**1. 数字化转型与生态系统**：

- MIS 的未来是生态系统协调。
