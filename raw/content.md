## 01. Information Systems Overview | 信息系统概述  <!-- ref: 01.Introduction.pdf -->

### Information Technology and Information Systems | 信息技术与信息系统  <!-- p=12 -->

[en]
**Information Technology (IT)**: all the hardware and software that a firm needs to use in order to achieve its business objectives. IT generally consists of computing technology, networking (communication) technology, and data management and analytics technology.

**Information System (IS)**: a set of interrelated components that collect, process, store, and distribute information to support decision making, coordination, and control. An IS helps managers and workers analyze problems, visualize complex subjects, and create new products.

**Data vs. Information**:
- `Data` are streams of raw facts.
- `Information` is data shaped into meaningful form.

**Functions of an Information System**:
An information system performs three basic activities—input, processing, and output—to produce the information organizations need. Feedback is output returned to appropriate people or activities in the organization to evaluate and refine input.

**Management Information System (MIS)**:
- Traditionally: IS that support middle-level management.
- Generally: Systems built with IT that facilitate management excellence (i.e., MIS = IS).
- Also refers to the disciplinary field that studies the construction, use, and impacts of information systems.
[/en]

[zh]
**信息技术（IT）**：企业为实现业务目标所需的所有硬件和软件。IT 通常包括计算技术、网络（通信）技术以及数据管理与分析技术。

**信息系统（IS）**：一组相互关联的组件，用于收集、处理、存储和分发信息，以支持决策、协调和控制。IS 帮助管理者和员工分析问题、可视化复杂主题并创造新产品。

**数据与信息**：
- `数据` 是原始事实的流。
- `信息` 是被赋予意义形态的数据。

**信息系统的功能**：
信息系统执行三项基本活动——输入、处理和输出——以产生组织所需的信息。反馈是返回给组织中适当人员或活动的输出，用于评估和优化输入。

**管理信息系统（MIS）**：
- 传统定义：支持组织中层管理的信息系统。
- 一般定义：用信息技术构建、促进卓越管理的系统（即 MIS = IS）。
- 也指研究信息系统的构建、使用和影响的学科领域。
[/en]

### Dimensions of Information Systems | 信息系统的维度  <!-- p=20 -->

[en]
An information system creates value for the firm as an organizational and management solution to challenges posed by the environment. Using IS effectively requires understanding three dimensions:

**1. Organization**:
Includes people, structure, business processes, politics, and culture. Organizations have a hierarchy of authority: senior management → middle management → operational management → knowledge workers → data workers → production/service workers. Business functions include sales and marketing, human resources, finance and accounting, and manufacturing and production.

**2. Management**:
Managers set organizational strategy for responding to business challenges and act creatively to create new products/services or occasionally re-create the organization.

**3. Technology**:
Includes computer hardware and software, data management and analytics technology, and networking/telecommunications technology. IT infrastructure provides the platform that the system is built on.
[/en]

[zh]
信息系统作为应对环境挑战的组织和管理解决方案，为企业创造价值。有效使用 IS 需要理解三个维度：

**1. 组织**：
包括人员、结构、业务流程、政治和文化。组织拥有权威层级：高层管理 → 中层管理 → 运营管理 → 知识工作者 → 数据工作者 → 生产/服务工作者。业务职能包括销售与市场、人力资源、财务与会计、制造与生产。

**2. 管理**：
管理者制定应对业务挑战的组织战略，并创造性地采取行动来创造新产品/服务，或偶尔重塑组织。

**3. 技术**：
包括计算机硬件和软件、数据管理与分析技术、网络/电信技术。IT 基础设施提供了系统构建的平台。
[/en]

### Organizational Structures | 组织结构类型  <!-- p=26 -->

[en]
**Mintzberg's Five Organizational Structures**:

| Type | Description | Examples |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Entrepreneurial Structure | Young, small firm in fast-changing environment; simple structure managed by a single CEO | Small start-up |
| Machine Bureaucracy | Large bureaucracy in slowly changing environment; standard products; centralized management | Midsize manufacturing firm |
| Divisionalized Bureaucracy | Multiple machine bureaucracies producing different products, topped by central HQ | Large diversified firms |
| Professional Bureaucracy | Knowledge-based; expertise-dependent; weak centralized authority | Law firms, hospitals, schools |
| Adhocracy | Task force; rapidly changing environments; multidisciplinary teams; weak central management | Consulting firms |

**Key Insight**: Organizations = Organizational Structures + Business Processes (类比：程序 = 数据结构 + 算法).
[/en]

[zh]
**明茨伯格的五种组织结构**：

| 类型 | 描述 | 示例 |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 创业型结构 | 小型年轻企业，快速变化环境，由单一 CEO 管理 | 小型初创公司 |
| 职能式层级结构 | 大型科层组织，缓慢变化环境，标准化产品，集中管理 | 中型制造企业 |
| 事业部式层级结构 | 多个职能式层级结构组合，生产不同产品，统一由总部管理 | 大型多元化公司 |

| 民主式结构 | 任务小组，快速响应变化，多学科团队，弱集中管理 | 咨询公司 |

**关键洞察**：组织 = 组织结构 + 业务流程（类比：程序 = 数据结构 + 算法）。
[/en]

### Organizational Impacts of Information Systems | 信息系统对组织的影响  <!-- p=29 -->

[en]
IS can impact all features of an organization:

| Feature | IS Impact |
|:---|:---|
| Goals (Strategy) | Support existing goals/strategy; Enable new goals/strategy |
| Organizational Structure | Reinforce existing structure; Enable more efficient structure |
| Business Processes | Increase efficiency of existing processes; Enable entirely new processes |
| Organizational Politics | May cause changes and conflicts in power, resources, and order |
| Organizational Culture | May threaten commonly held cultural assumptions |
| Organizational Environments | Help sense and respond to environmental changes |

**Considerations when building IS**:
- Is the system aligned with the goals of the firm?
- Does the system fit with the organizational structure? Is changing the structure beneficial?
- How will business processes be changed?
- What conflicts of interest, power, resources will arise?
- Will the system threaten cultural assumptions?
- How may the system sense and respond to environmental changes?
[/en]

[zh]
IS 可以影响组织的所有特征：

| 特征 | IS 的影响 |
|:---|:---|
| 目标（战略） | 支持现有战略；促成新战略 |
| 组织结构 | 巩固现有结构；促成更高效的结构 |
| 业务流程 | 提高现有流程效率；促成全新流程 |
| 组织政治 | 可能引发权力、资源和秩序的变革与冲突 |
| 组织文化 | 可能挑战普遍认同的文化假设 |

**构建 IS 时需要考虑的问题**：
- 系统是否与公司的目标一致？
- 系统是否与组织结构匹配？改变结构是否有益？
- 业务流程将如何改变？
- 会产生哪些利益、权力、资源的冲突？
- 系统是否会挑战组织文化假设？
- 系统如何感知和响应组织环境的变化？
[/en]

### The Emerging Digital Firm | 新兴的数字化企业  <!-- p=34 -->

[en]
In a **fully digital firm**:
- Significant business relationships are digitally enabled and mediated
- Core business processes are accomplished through digital networks
- Key corporate assets are managed digitally
- Digital firms offer greater flexibility: **time shifting** and **space shifting**

A digital firm has established interdependence between its ability to use IT and its ability to implement corporate strategies and achieve corporate goals.

**Complementary Assets**: Investing in IT alone does not guarantee good returns. Firms need the right business model and complementary assets (organizational and management capital) — the organization and management dimensions are as important as the technology dimension.
[/en]

[zh]
在**完全数字化企业**中：
- 重要的业务关系通过数字方式实现和协调
- 核心业务流程通过数字网络完成
- 关键企业资产通过数字方式管理
- 数字化企业提供更大的灵活性：**时间转移**和**空间转移**

数字化企业在其使用 IT 的能力与实施公司战略及实现公司目标的能力之间建立了相互依赖关系。

**互补性资产**：仅靠 IT 投资不能保证良好回报。企业需要正确的业务模式和互补性资产（组织和管理资本）——组织和管理维度与技术维度同样重要。

### Information Systems, Organizations, and Strategy | 信息系统、组织与战略  <!-- p=36 -->

[en]
**What is an Organization?**
- **Technical definition**: A formal social structure that processes resources from the environment to produce outputs; a formal legal entity with internal rules and procedures, as well as a social structure.
- **Behavioral definition**: A collection of rights, privileges, obligations, and responsibilities that is delicately balanced over time through conflict and conflict resolution.

**Features of Organizations**:
- Goals (Strategy)
- Organizational Structure
- Business Processes
- Organizational Politics
- Organizational Culture
- Organizational Environments

**Porter's Competitive Forces Model**:
Five competitive forces shape the fate of a firm:
1. Traditional competitors
2. New market entrants
3. Substitute products and services
4. Customers
5. Suppliers

**Strategies for Dealing with Competitive Forces**:
- **Low-cost leadership**: Produce at lower prices (e.g., Walmart, Xiaomi)
- **Product differentiation**: Enable new products/services, change customer experience (e.g., Google, Apple, DJI)
- **Focus on market niche**: Specialize in a single market niche (e.g., Transsion, Pop Mart)
- **Strengthen customer and supplier intimacy**: Develop strong ties and loyalty, increase switching costs (e.g., Toyota, SHEIN)

**The Business Value Chain Model**:
Views the firm as a series of activities that add value to products or services. Primary activities vs. support activities.
[/en]

[zh]
**什么是组织？**
- **技术定义**：从环境处理资源以产生输出的正式社会结构；具有内部规则和程序以及社会结构的正式法律实体。
- **行为定义**：在一段时间内通过冲突和冲突解决微妙平衡的权利、特权、义务和责任的集合。

**组织特征**：
- 目标（战略）
- 组织结构
- 业务流程
- 组织政治
- 组织文化
- 组织环境

**波特的竞争力量模型**：
五种竞争力量决定了企业的命运：
1. 传统竞争者
2. 新市场进入者
3. 替代产品和服务
4. 客户
5. 供应商

**应对竞争力量的策略**：
- **低成本领导**：以更低价格生产（如沃尔玛、小米）
- **产品差异化**：创造新产品/服务，改变客户体验（如谷歌、苹果、大疆）
- **聚焦市场利基**：专注于单一市场细分（如传音、泡泡玛特）
- **加强客户与供应商亲密性**：建立牢固关系和忠诚度，提高转换成本（如丰田、希音）

**业务价值链模型**：
将企业视为一系列为产品或服务增加价值的活动。主要活动 vs. 支持活动。
[/en]

### Types of Information Systems | 信息系统的类型  <!-- p=54 -->

[en]
**Systems for Different Management Levels**:
| Level | System Type | Description |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Senior Management | Executive Support Systems (ESS) | Address nonroutine decisions requiring judgment, evaluation, and insight |
| Middle Management | Management Information Systems (MIS) + Decision Support Systems (DSS) | MIS provides reports on current performance; DSS supports nonroutine decision making |
| Operational Management | Transaction Processing Systems (TPS) | Perform and record daily routine transactions |

**Enterprise Applications**:
Systems for linking the enterprise that span functional areas, execute business processes across the firm, and include all levels of management. Four major applications:
- Enterprise Systems (ERP)
- Supply Chain Management Systems (SCM)
- Customer Relationship Management Systems (CRM)
- Knowledge Management Systems (KMS)

**E-business, E-commerce, and E-government**:
- **E-business**: Use of digital technology and Internet to drive major business processes.
- **E-commerce**: Subset of e-business; buying and selling goods and services through the Internet.
- **E-government**: Using Internet technology to deliver information and services to citizens, employees, and businesses.
[/en]

[zh]
**不同管理层级的系统**：
| 层级 | 系统类型 | 描述 |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 高层管理 | 主管支持系统（ESS） | 处理需要判断、评估和洞察的非例行决策 |
| 中层管理 | 管理信息系统（MIS）+ 决策支持系统（DSS） | MIS 提供当前绩效报告；DSS 支持非例行决策 |
| 运营管理 | 事务处理系统（TPS） | 执行和记录日常例行事务 |

**企业应用系统**：
连接企业的系统，跨越职能领域，跨整个公司执行业务流程，包括所有管理层级。四大主要应用：
- 企业系统（ERP）
- 供应链管理系统（SCM）
- 客户关系管理系统（CRM）
- 知识管理系统（KMS）

**电子商务、电子商贸与电子政务**：
- **电子商务（广义）**：使用数字技术和互联网驱动主要业务流程。
- **电子商贸（狭义）**：电子商务的子集；通过互联网买卖商品和服务。
- **电子政务**：使用互联网技术向公民、员工和企业提供信息和服务。
[/en]

### Detailed Look: TPS, MIS, DSS, ESS | 详解各层级系统  <!-- p=55 -->

[en]
**Transaction Processing Systems (TPS)**:
- Serve operational managers and staff.
- Perform and record daily routine transactions (e.g., sales order entry, payroll, shipping).
- Allow managers to monitor status of operations and relations with external environment.
- Serve predefined, structured goals and decision making.

**Management Information Systems (MIS)**:
- Serve middle management.
- Provide reports on firm's current performance, based on data from TPS.
- Provide answers to routine questions with predefined procedures.
- Typically have little analytic capability.

**Decision Support Systems (DSS)**:
- Serve middle management.
- Support nonroutine decision making (e.g., "What is the impact on production schedule if December sales doubled?").
- May use external information as well as TPS/MIS data.

**Executive Support Systems (ESS)**:
- Support senior management.
- Address nonroutine decisions requiring judgment, evaluation, and insight.
- Incorporate external events (e.g., new tax laws, competitors) as well as summarized internal information.
- Example: Digital dashboard with real-time view of firm's financial performance.
[/en]

[zh]
**事务处理系统（TPS）**：
- 服务运营管理人员和一线员工。
- 执行和记录日常例行事务（如销售订单录入、工资发放、发货）。
- 使管理者能监控运营状态和与外部环境的关系。
- 服务于预定义的结构化目标和决策。

**管理信息系统（MIS）**：
- 服务中层管理。
- 基于 TPS 数据提供公司当前绩效报告。
- 以预定义程序回答例行问题。
- 通常分析能力有限。

**决策支持系统（DSS）**：
- 服务中层管理。
- 支持非例行决策（如"如果 12 月销售额翻倍，对生产计划有何影响？"）。
- 可使用外部信息和 TPS/MIS 数据。

**主管支持系统（ESS）**：
- 支持高层管理。
- 处理需要判断、评估和洞察的非例行决策。
- 整合外部事件（如新税法、竞争对手）和内部汇总信息。
- 示例：实时显示公司财务绩效的数字仪表板。
[/en]

### Technology Drivers of Infrastructure Evolution | 基础设施演进的技术驱动因素  <!-- p=8 -->

[en]
Key technology drivers:
- **Moore's Law**: Computing power doubles every 2 years.
- **Law of Mass Digital Storage**: The amount of data being stored each year doubles.
- **Metcalfe's Law**: The value or power of a network grows exponentially as a function of the number of network members.
- **Declining communication costs and the Internet**: Exponential growth in the size of the Internet.
- **Standards and network effects**: Specifications that establish product compatibility.
[/en]

### Data, Analytics, and Intelligence | 数据、分析与智能  <!-- p=14 -->

[en]
**Database Management Systems (DBMS)**:
Software that permits an organization to centralize data, manage them efficiently, and provide access to stored data by application programs. A database is a collection of data organized to serve many applications efficiently by centralizing data and controlling redundant data.

**Emerging Database Forms**:
- **NoSQL (Non-relational databases)**: More flexible data model; data sets stored across distributed machines; easier to scale; handles large volumes of unstructured and structured data.
- **Vector Databases**: Stores unstructured data (text, images, audio) as high-dimensional numerical arrays (embeddings) generated by neural networks. Executes semantic similarity search to find data with similar meaning. Functions as the critical "long-term memory" infrastructure for Generative AI, fundamentally powering Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG).
- **Databases in the Cloud**: Examples include Aliyun RDS, Amazon AWS Database, Microsoft SQL Azure Database. Benefits: better response to real-time demand, low costs.

**Information Utilization Levels**:
- Data Warehouse: Stores current and historical data from many core operational transaction systems. Provides analysis and reporting tools.
- Analytical Tools:
  - Multidimensional data analysis (OLAP - Online Analytical Processing)

### Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) | 企业资源计划  <!-- p=6 -->

[en]
**Evolution of Manufacturing System Management**:
- **ROP (Reorder Point)**: Early inventory management method (1940s).
- **MRP (Material Requirement Planning)**: Production planning, scheduling, and inventory control system to manage manufacturing processes. Solves: minimal completion time, minimal cost, maximal production using Bill of Materials (BOM).
- **MRP II (Manufacturing Resource Planning)**: Method for effective planning of all resources directly related to production (Money, Manpower, Materials, production capacity). Adds "what-if" simulation.
- **ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)**: Business management software/system, usually a suite of integrated applications that collect, store, manage, and interpret data from many business activities including product planning, manufacturing, marketing and sales, inventory management, shipping and payment.

**Key Features of ERP**:
- Suite of integrated software modules and a common central database.
- Collects data from many divisions of the firm for use in nearly all internal business activities.
- Information entered in one process is immediately available for other processes.
- Integration: Functional areas are integrated.
- Centralization: Databases are centralized.

**Major ERP Vendors**: SAP, Oracle (PeopleSoft), Microsoft, Sage, Epicor, Infor, Odoo (open source), Yonyou (用友), Kingdee (金蝶).

**Implementation of ERP**:
- Built around thousands of predefined business processes reflecting best practices.
- To implement, firms: select functions, conduct Business Process Re-engineering (BPR), map business processes to software processes, use configuration tables for customizing.

**Risks of ERP**:
- Conflicts with business strategy ("best practices" may not be best for you)
- Huge investments
- Conflicts with organizational culture and politics (organizational resistance)
- Changes in employee responsibilities
- Loss of flexibility

**Key Success Factors for Implementing ERP**:
- Organization is prepared for change.
- Initiative is seen as business imperative.
- Executive leaders are active and visible in support.
- Resources are available.
- Good packaged system is used and customized.
- Users are trained before implementation.
- Stress the enterprise, not the system.
- General manager acts as mediator.
[/en]

### MRP Details and Evolution | MRP 详解与演进  <!-- p=27 -->

[en]
**MRP (Material Requirement Planning)** — three simultaneous objectives:
1. **Material**: Ensure materials are available for production and products are available for delivery.
2. **Requirement**: Maintain the lowest possible material and product levels in store.
3. **Plan**: Plan manufacturing activities, delivery schedules, and purchasing activities.

**Bill of Materials (BOM)**: A comprehensive list of parts, assemblies, and other materials required to create a product. MRP uses BOM to back-calculate from target → required inputs. Theoretic foundation: **Operations Research (OR, 运筹学)**.

**Limitations of MRP**:
- Focuses only on materials; assumes no constraints on production capacity, procurement, or manpower.
- No cost information integrated.
- Lack of capability to change based on feedback.
- Poor data integrity (low quality data).

**MRP II (Manufacturing Resource Planning)** improvements:
- Addresses all resources: Money, Manpower, Materials, production capacity.
- Adds operational planning in financial units.
- Has simulation capability for "what-if" questions.

**Why MRP II → ERP?** The ultimate goal is to maximize value (profit), not just optimize production. In an Abundance Economy (post-1990s/2000s), marketing, financing, procurement, logistics, sales, and HR become as important as production itself.
[/en]

[zh]
**MRP（物料需求计划）**——三个同步目标：
1. **物料**：确保生产有料可用，产品可供交付。
2. **需求**：维持尽可能低的物料和产品库存水平。
3. **计划**：规划制造活动、交货日程和采购活动。

**物料清单（BOM）**：创建产品所需的零件、组件和其他材料的综合清单。MRP 使用 BOM 从目标向后计算所需输入。理论基础：**运筹学（OR）**。

**MRP 的局限性**：
- 仅关注物料；假设生产能力、采购、人力无约束。
- 通常不整合成本信息。
- 缺乏根据反馈进行调整的能力。
- 数据完整性差（数据质量低）。

**MRP II（制造资源计划）的改进**：
- 覆盖所有资源：资金、人力、材料、生产能力。
- 新增财务单位的运营计划。
- 具备"假设"问题的模拟能力。

**为何 MRP II → ERP？** 企业终极目标是最大化价值（利润），而不仅仅是优化生产。在充裕经济（1990年代/2000年代之后）中，营销、融资、采购、物流、销售和 HR 变得与生产同等重要。
[/en]

### Supply Chain Management (SCM) | 供应链管理  <!-- p=31 -->

[en]
**Supply Chain**: Network of organizations and processes for procuring materials, transforming them into products, and distributing the products.
- Upstream supply chain: Firm's suppliers, suppliers' suppliers.
- Downstream supply chain: Organizations and processes for delivering products to customers.
- Internal supply chain.

**Bullwhip Effect (牛鞭效应)**: Information about product demand gets distorted as it passes from one entity to the next across the supply chain. Causes: uncertain customer orders, replenishment delays, order batching, price fluctuations, rationing and shortage gaming.

**Vendor Managed Inventory (VMI)**: Suppliers manage inventory levels for customers (e.g., P&G and Walmart). Benefits: reduced inventory costs, improved efficiency.

**Push Strategy vs. Pull Strategy**:
- Push ("sell what we make"): Work release based on downstream demand forecasts. Proactive, but forecast uncertainty.
- Pull ("make what we sell"): Work release based on actual demand. Reactive, but delayed information.

**Push-Pull Strategy**: Hybrid approach. Early stages use push based on aggregated forecasts; final production uses pull based on specific customer demand.

**Supply Chain Management Systems**:
- Supply Chain Planning Systems: Model existing supply chain, enable demand planning, optimize sourcing/manufacturing plans, establish inventory levels, identify transportation modes.
- Supply Chain Execution Systems: Manage flow of products through distribution centers and warehouses.
[/en]

[zh]
**供应链**：采购材料、将其转化为产品并分销产品的组织和流程网络。
- 上游供应链：企业的供应商、供应商的供应商。
- 下游供应链：负责将产品交付给客户的组织和流程。
- 内部供应链。

**牛鞭效应**：产品需求信息在供应链中从一个实体传递到下一个实体时被扭曲。原因：客户订单不确定、补货延迟、批量订购、价格波动、配给与短缺博弈。

**供应商管理库存（VMI）**：供应商为客户管理库存水平（如宝洁与沃尔玛）。收益：降低库存成本，提高效率。

**推式策略 vs. 拉式策略**：
- 推式（"生产什么卖什么"）：基于下游需求预测发布工作。主动，但预测存在不确定性。
- 拉式（"卖什么生产什么"）：基于实际需求发布工作。被动，但信息存在延迟。

**推-拉混合策略**：混合方法。早期阶段使用基于汇总预测的推式；最终生产使用基于特定客户需求的拉式。

**供应链效率与准时制**：
- 供应链低效可浪费高达 25% 的运营费用。
- **准时制（Just-in-Time, JIT）策略**：组件在需要时到达，成品在离开装配线后发货。条件：关于客户需求的完美信息。
- 现实中的不确定性：需求不确定、供应商延迟发货、缺陷零件、生产中断 → 需要**安全库存**作为缓冲。

**供应商管理库存（VMI）经典案例——宝洁与沃尔玛**：
- 1980年：沃尔玛圣路易斯门店致电宝洁，请求自动持续补货货架上的帮宝适。
- 宝洁经理设计了沃尔玛库存数据库与宝洁生产仓库数据库的连接。
- 当货架几乎空时，供应商自动发货，每月结算付款。
- 1987年：宝洁将系统从帮宝适扩展到所有产品。
- 沃尔玛的收益：节省约 20% 的库存持有成本。
- 宝洁的收益：实时获取沃尔玛数据，每天精准配送匹配预测销量，将库存和缺货控制在最低水平。

**牛鞭效应复现——啤酒分销游戏数据**：

| 情景 | 总链成本 | 成本指数 | 需求放大率(%) |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| 基准案例 | 3358 | 1.47 | 900 |
| 无订单延迟 | 1944 | 0.85 | 500 |
| 生产者-零售商间无中间商 | 939 | 0.82 | 350 |
| 生产者可获取消费者需求数据 | 2295 | 1.01 | 425 |
| 所有阶段可获取消费者需求数据 | 1293 | 0.57 | 200 |

核心结论：信息共享和减少中间环节显著降低牛鞭效应。
[/en]

### Push-Pull Strategy and Product Types | 推拉策略与产品类型  <!-- p=48 -->

[en]
**Trade-off between Efficiency and Responsiveness**:
| Product Type | Characteristics | Strategy |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Functional Products | Stable, predictable demand; long life cycles; low profit margins | Push (efficiency-focused) |
| Innovative Products | Unpredictable demand; short life cycles; high variety; higher margins | Pull (responsiveness-focused) |

| 产品类型 | 特征 | 策略 |
|:---|:---|:---|

### Customer Relationship Management (CRM) | 客户关系管理  <!-- p=54 -->

[en]
**CRM Definition**: An integrated system for identifying, acquiring, and maintaining customers; supporting and enhancing marketing, sales, and service. Captures and integrates customer data from all over the organization, consolidates and analyzes it, and distributes it to various systems and customer touch points.

**Functional Types of CRM**:
| Type | Description |
|:---|:---|
| Operational CRM | Customer-facing applications: sales force automation (SFA), call center and customer service support, marketing automation |
| Analytical CRM | Based on data warehouses; analyzes customer data (OLAP, data mining) for customer lifetime value (CLTV) |
| Collaborative CRM | Collects, organizes, and shares customer data across multiple teams; improves customer experience with unified messaging |

**CRM Components**:
- Sales: Sales prospect (lead) and contact information, sales quote generation.
- Service: Assigning and managing customer service requests, web-based self-service.
- Marketing: Capturing prospect and customer data, scheduling direct-marketing, cross-selling, up-selling, down-selling.

**CRM Paradox ("Dark side of CRM")**: Treating customers differently (segmentation) may cause perceptions of unfairness, dissatisfaction, and mistrust (e.g., Amazon's dynamic pricing controversy).

**Trends in CRM**:
- Social media for inbound marketing: BestBuy's personalized customer service; Ford used text mining on tweets to help design SUV trunk features.
- Cloud computing and SaaS-based CRM: e.g., Salesforce.com.
- Mobile devices and IoT as data sources: EMC² helped Audi optimize car models based on GPS, braking data, driving habits; iQIYI used sequence mining on user click/drag data to edit shows.
- Integration with ERP/SCM: enables Total Life Cycle Immersive Marketing.

### Why E-commerce is Different | 电子商务为何不同  <!-- p=4 -->

[en]
**Unique Features of E-commerce**:
- Ubiquity: Marketspace is virtual; transaction costs reduced.
- Global reach: Transactions cross cultural and national boundaries.

- **Disintermediation**: Removing intermediary layers lowers final cost to customer.
- **Two-sided Markets (Multi-sided platforms)**: Technology enables connecting different users to create value (e.g., Google, Taobao, Uber).

**Digital Goods are Different**:
Digital goods are goods that can be delivered over a digital network:
- Information products (e-books, news, music, video)
- Symbols and data records (tickets, financial instruments)
- Services (messaging, business transactions, entertainment)

**Properties of Digital Goods**:
- Non-depletion (can be used infinitely)
- Variable (can be customized)
- Replicable (easy to copy)
- Individualized value
- Network externalities
- Economy of scale (high fixed costs, near-zero marginal costs)

**New Possibilities Enabled by Digital Goods**:
- Personalization and Long-tail
- Differential pricing
- Bundling
- New forms of value creation (two-sided markets)

**The Long Tail**: In digital markets, the top 20% products bring 80% revenues, but the remaining 80% (the "tail") collectively can be just as profitable due to low storage and distribution costs.

**Differential Pricing Strategies**:
- Personalization (first-degree price discrimination)
- Versioning (second-degree price discrimination)
- Group pricing (third-degree price discrimination)

**Bundling**: Selling multiple products together (e.g., Microsoft Office). Reasons: complementarity, competition for markets, reshaping demand curve.

### From Bundling to Subscription Economy | 从捆绑到订阅经济  <!-- p=23 -->

[en]
**The Shift in Digital Economics**:
- **Traditional Bundling**: Selling discrete products together to capture consumer surplus in a single transaction (e.g., buying a CD-ROM of Microsoft Office 2007).
- **Subscription Economy**: Selling continuous access to a continuously updated platform (e.g., Microsoft 365, Adobe Creative Cloud, ChatGPT Plus).
- **Core mechanism**: Still bundling — but in the *time dimension*.  
- **Lock-in Effect & Switching Costs**: As users accumulate data and habits within a cloud ecosystem, leaving becomes prohibitively expensive.
- **Democratization + Extraction**: Subscriptions lower the initial barrier but allow firms to extract greater total value over the customer lifetime.
[/en]

[zh]
**数字经济的转变**：
- **传统捆绑**：将离散产品一起销售以在单次交易中获取消费者剩余（如购买一张 Microsoft Office 2007 CD-ROM）。
- **订阅经济**：销售对持续更新平台的持续访问（如 Microsoft 365、Adobe Creative Cloud、ChatGPT Plus）。
- **核心机制**：仍然是捆绑——但是在*时间维度*上。
- **锁定效应与转换成本**：当用户在云生态系统中积累数据和习惯后，离开变得代价高昂。
- **民主化 + 价值提取**：订阅降低了初始门槛，但使企业能够在整个客户生命周期中获取更大的总价值。
[/en]

### Knowledge Management | 知识管理  <!-- p=27 -->

[en]
**Uses of Knowledge Management**:
1. Employees find answers to questions.
2. Train employees on new processes.
3. Speed up time-to-productivity and time-to-value.
4. Collaborate (e.g., cross-functional report writing).
5. Tap into expert knowledge.
6. Easily share and find information.

**Explicit vs. Tacit Knowledge**:
- **Explicit Knowledge**: Knowledge that has been documented. Structured (enterprise applications) or semi-structured/unstructured (main content of KMS).
- **Tacit Knowledge**: Knowledge residing in the minds of employees that has not been documented. Converted through collaborative environments (Communities of Practice, CoP) or AI.

**Major Types of Knowledge Management Systems**:
- Enterprise-wide Knowledge Management Systems: Enterprise Content Management (ECM), collaboration and social tools, learning management systems.
- Knowledge Work Systems (KWS): Computer-Aided Design (CAD), virtual reality for scientists, engineers, knowledge workers.
- Intelligent Techniques: Data mining, neural networks, expert systems, case-based reasoning, fuzzy logic, genetic algorithms, intelligent agents.

**Enterprise-Wide KM Systems in Detail**:
- **Enterprise Content Management (ECM)**: Help capture, store, retrieve, distribute, preserve documents and semi-structured knowledge. Include digital asset management systems.
- **Collaboration & Social Tools**: Blogs, wikis, social networking tools.
- **Learning Management Systems (LMS)**: For training and managing learning activities.
- **Key Challenge**: Developing taxonomy; alternatives: tagging, search engines.
- **Major Vendors**: Alfresco, Box Platform, Hyland OnBase, IBM Cloud Pak, iManage Work, Microsoft 365, and Chinese vendors.

**Knowledge Work Systems & Technologies**:
- **Knowledge Workers**: Researchers, designers, architects, scientists, engineers. Three key roles: ① keeping organization current in knowledge; ② serving as internal consultants; ③ acting as change agents.
- **CAD (Computer-Aided Design)**: Creating engineering/architectural designs; 3D printing.
- **VR/AR Systems**: Virtual and augmented reality for simulation and design.
- **Collaboration Systems**: Document collaboration (Google Docs, Tencent Docs, Dropbox); Developer collaboration (GitHub, GitLab); Project management (Notion, Confluence).

**Intelligent Techniques**:
- **Capturing Expert Knowledge**: Rule-based (expert systems, fuzzy logic); Case-based (case-based reasoning).
- **Discovering Knowledge**: Machine learning (neural networks, genetic algorithms, LLMs like ChatGPT, DeepSeek); Data mining (associations, sequences, classification, clustering, forecasting).
- **Automating Tasks**: Intelligent agents — software that performs tasks autonomously on behalf of users.

**LLMs in Knowledge Management**:
Four current approaches: prompt engineering, fine-tuning, RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation), contextualized AI agents.

**Challenges of LLMs in Knowledge Management**:
- Hallucination: Generating plausible but factually incorrect information.
- Data Privacy and Security: Risk of exposing sensitive corporate data.
- Dynamic Access Control: RAG and AI agents must respect user role boundaries.
- Cost and Maintenance: High computational costs, overhead of maintaining vector databases.
[/en]

- **模型驱动（现代 AI/智能体 BI）**：预测性与指导性。输出：情景模拟、算法推荐、自主行动。人的角色：战略监督。
[/en]

### User-Designer Communication Gap | 用户-设计者沟通鸿沟  <!-- p=38 -->

[en]
A major cause of IS project failure. IS specialists have a technical orientation; users prefer systems oriented to solving business problems.

| User Concerns | Designer Concerns |
|:---|:---|

| What new procedures do we need to enter data into the system? | Where will the data be stored? What's the most efficient way to store them? |
| How will the operation of the system change employees' daily routines? | What technologies should we use to secure the data? |

**Change Agent**: The systems analyst's role is to bridge this gap, redefining the configurations, interactions, job activities, and power relationships of organizational groups.
[/en]

[zh]

## 06. Systems Analysis Fundamentals | 系统分析基础  <!-- ref: 06.Systems_Analysis (1).pdf -->

### The Need for Systems Analysis and Design | 系统分析与设计的必要性  <!-- p=6 -->

[en]
**Systems analysis and design** is the process to analyze, design, and implement improvements through the use of computerized information systems in support of users and the functioning of businesses.

Why is it needed?
- To align systems (solutions) with business targets.
- To structure and guide systems development, which is a costly endeavor.
- To involve users.

**Failure Case Examples**:
- **Lidl's €500 Million SAP Disaster (2018)**: Inadequate requirements understanding (inventory at purchase prices vs. retail prices); refusal to re-engineer business processes; massive customizations that made the system slow and unstable.
- **Moutai's Data Middle Platform Collapse (2020)**: Ignored organizational culture (agile platform vs. rigid SOE); over-centralization; excluded line-of-business managers from requirements gathering.
- **MissFresh Supply-Chain Algorithm Bankruptcy (2022)**: Underestimated demand volatility and spoilage risks; no margin-aware cost modeling; prioritized technical feasibility over economic feasibility.
[/en]

[zh]
**系统分析与设计**是通过使用计算机化信息系统来分析、设计和实施改进以支持用户和企业运营的过程。

为什么需要？
- 使系统（解决方案）与业务目标对齐。
- 构建和指导系统开发，这是一项成本高昂的工作。
- 让用户参与。

**失败案例**：
- **Lidl 的 5 亿欧元 SAP 灾难（2018）**：需求理解不足（按采购价 vs. 零售价计算库存）；拒绝重新设计业务流程；大量定制导致系统缓慢和不稳定。
- **茅台数据中台崩溃（2020）**：忽视组织文化（敏捷平台 vs. 僵化的国企）；过度集中化；排除了业务部门管理者的需求收集。
- **每日优鲜供应链算法破产（2022）**：低估需求波动和损耗风险；没有考虑利润率的成本建模；技术可行性优先于经济可行性。

### SDLC Phases in Detail | SDLC 各阶段详解  <!-- p=26 -->

[en]
**1. Identifying Problems, Opportunities, and Objectives**
- Activity: Interview user management, summarize knowledge, estimate scope, document results.
- Output: Feasibility report with problem definition and objective summaries.

**2. Determining Information Requirements**
- Activity: Interviewing, sampling, questionnaires, observe decision-maker's behavior/environment, prototyping. Learn the who, what, where, when, how, and why of the current system.
- Output: Understanding of how users accomplish work; complete information on people, goals, data, and procedures.

**3. Analyzing System Needs**
- Activity: Create DFDs, complete data dictionary, analyze structured decisions, prepare system proposal.
- Output: Recommendation on what should be done.

**4. Designing the Recommended System**
- Activity: Design data entry procedures, HCI, files/database, backup procedures.
- Output: Model of the actual system.

**5. Developing and Documenting Software**
- Activity: Work with programmers to develop software; work with users to develop documentation (procedure manuals, online help, FAQ, ReadMe).
- Output: Computer programs, system documentation.

**6. Testing and Maintaining the System**
- Activity: Test the IS; system maintenance; maintenance documentation.
- Output: Problems identified, updated programs, documentation.

**7. Implementing and Evaluating the System**
- Activity: Train users, plan conversion, review and evaluate system.
- Output: Trained personnel, installed system.
[/en]

[zh]
**1. 识别问题、机会与目标**
- 活动：访谈用户管理、汇总知识、估算范围、记录结果。
- 输出：包含问题定义和目标摘要的可行性报告。

**2. 确定信息需求**
- 活动：访谈、抽样、问卷、观察决策者行为与环境、原型开发。了解当前系统的 who, what, where, when, how, why。
- 输出：理解用户如何完成工作；获得人员、目标、数据和流程的完整信息。

**3. 分析系统需求**
- 活动：创建 DFD、完善数据字典、分析结构化决策、准备系统建议书。
- 输出：关于应如何处理的建议。

**4. 设计推荐系统**
- 活动：设计数据录入流程、人机界面、文件/数据库、备份流程。
- 输出：实际系统的模型。

**5. 开发与文档化软件**
- 活动：与程序员协作开发软件；与用户协作编写文档（操作手册、在线帮助、FAQ、ReadMe）。

### Understanding and Modeling Organizational Systems | 理解与建模组织系统  <!-- p=37 -->

[en]
**Three Modeling Techniques**:
1. **Context-Level Data Flow Diagrams (DFD)**: Dynamic modeling. Shows scope of system: what is included (processes) and what is external (entities). One process (0), external entities, major data flows.
2. **Entity-Relationship Model (ERD)**: Static modeling. Shows entities and their relationships (one-to-one, one-to-many, many-to-many).
3. **Use Case Modeling (UML)**: Functional modeling. Describes what the system does without describing how. Actors (external users/roles), use cases (tasks), relationships (communicates, includes, extends, generalizes). Accompanied by use case scenarios.

**Use Case Relationships**:
- **Communicates**: Actor connects to use case.
- **Include (<<include>>)**: A use case contains behavior common to multiple use cases.
- **Extend (<<extend>>)**: A use case handles exceptions from the basic use case.
- **Generalizes**: One "thing" is more general than another.
[/en]

[zh]
**三种建模技术**：
1. **上下文级数据流图（DFD）**：动态建模。显示系统范围：包含的内容（过程）和外部内容（实体）。一个过程（0）、外部实体、主要数据流。
2. **实体-关系模型（ERD）**：静态建模。显示实体及其关系（一对一、一对多、多对多）。
3. **用例建模（UML）**：功能建模。描述系统做什么而不描述如何做。参与者（外部用户/角色）、用例（任务）、关系（通讯、包含、扩展、泛化）。伴随有用例场景。

**用例关系**：
- **通讯**：参与者连接到用例。
- **包含（<<include>>）**：一个用例包含多个用例共有的行为。
- **扩展（<<extend>>）**：一个用例处理基本用例的异常。
- **泛化**：一个"事物"比另一个更一般化。

### Interactive Methods | 交互式方法  <!-- p=6 -->

[en]
**Three Interactive Methods to Elicit Human Information Requirements**:
1. **Interviewing**: Important for collecting data on human and system requirements. Reveals opinions, feelings, goals, HCI concerns.

### Scrum in Detail | Scrum 详解  <!-- p=12 -->

[en]
Scrum is founded on empirical process control theory with three pillars:
- **Transparency**: Process and work must be visible.
- **Inspection**: Frequent inspection of artifacts and progress.
- **Adaptation**: Adjust process when it deviates from acceptable limits.

**Roles**:
- **Product Owner**: Represents stakeholders; manages Product Backlog; maximizes value.
- **Scrum Master**: Coaches team; removes impediments; ensures Scrum is understood.
- **Developers**: Cross-functional, self-organizing; deliver potentially releasable Increment each Sprint.

**Artifacts**:
- **Product Backlog**: Ordered list of everything needed; single source of work.
- **Sprint Backlog**: Selected items + plan for delivering them.
- **Increment**: Usable, working product produced by the end of sprint.

**Events**:
- **Sprint Planning**: Define what will be done (max 8 hours for 1-month sprint).
- **Daily Scrum**: 15-minute daily stand-up to align progress.
- **Sprint Review**: Demo work to stakeholders for feedback.
- **Sprint Retrospective**: Reflect on process and create improvement plan.
[/en]

[zh]
Scrum 建立在经验过程控制理论之上，三大支柱：
- **透明性**：过程和所做工作必须可见。
- **检视**：频繁检查工件和进展。
- **调适**：当过程偏离可接受限度时进行调整。

**角色**：
- **产品负责人**：代表利益相关者；管理产品待办列表；最大化价值。
- **Scrum 主管**：指导团队；消除障碍；确保 Scrum 被正确理解和执行。
- **开发人员**：跨职能、自组织；每个冲刺交付潜在可发布增量。

**工件**：
- **产品待办列表**：所需内容的排序列表；唯一工作来源。
- **冲刺待办列表**：选中的条目 + 交付计划。
- **增量**：冲刺结束时产生的可用、可工作的产品。

**事件**：
- **冲刺规划**：定义将要做什么（1 个月冲刺最多 8 小时）。
- **每日站会**：15 分钟每日同步进展。
- **冲刺评审**：向利益相关者展示工作以获取反馈。
- **冲刺回顾**：反思过程并制定改进计划。

### Data Flow Diagrams | 数据流图  <!-- p=18 -->

[en]
**Data Flow Diagram (DFD)**: Graphically characterizes data processes and flows in a business system. Depicts system inputs, processes, outputs.

**Basic Symbols**:
- Process (rounded rectangle)
- Data flow (arrow)
- Data store (open-ended rectangle)
- External entity (square or rectangle)

**Basic Rules**:
- Must have at least one process.
- No freestanding objects.
- Process must have both input and output data flow.
- Data store must connect to at least one process.
- External entities should not connect to each other.

**Developing DFDs – Top-Down Approach**:
1. Create Context Diagram (highest level; one process numbered 0; all external entities and major data flows)
2. Draw Diagram 0 (next level; up to 9 processes; major data stores)
3. Create Child Diagrams (explode each process; lower level number = parent process number; primitive process = not exploded)
4. Check for errors
5. Develop physical DFD from logical DFD
6. Partition the physical DFD

**Logical vs. Physical DFD**:
- Logical: how business operates (business activities)
- Physical: how system will be implemented (programs, modules, manual procedures, physical files)

**Event Modeling**: Input flow from external entity is a trigger that starts process activities. Event response table maps events to triggers, activities, responses, destinations.

**Partitioning**: Divide DFD into collections of manual procedures and computer programs based on: different user groups, timing, similar tasks, efficiency, consistency of data, security.
[/en]

[zh]
**数据流图（DFD）**：图形化表征业务系统中的数据过程和流。描绘系统输入、过程、输出。

**基本符号**：
- 过程（圆角矩形）
- 数据流（箭头）
- 数据存储（开口矩形）
- 外部实体（正方形或矩形）

**基本规则**：
- 必须至少有一个过程。
- 不能有独立对象。
- 过程必须有输入和输出数据流。
- 数据存储必须连接到至少一个过程。
- 外部实体不应相互连接。

**开发 DFD——自顶向下方法**：
1. 创建上下文图（最高层；一个过程编号为0；所有外部实体和主要数据流）

3. 创建子图（展开每个过程；子图编号 = 父过程编号；原语过程 = 未展开）
4. 检查错误
5. 从逻辑 DFD 开发物理 DFD
6. 划分物理 DFD

**逻辑 DFD 与物理 DFD**：
- 逻辑：业务如何运作（业务活动）
- 物理：系统将如何实施（程序、模块、手工流程、物理文件）

### Process Specifications and Structured Decisions | 过程规范与结构化决策  <!-- p=18 -->

[en]
**Process Specifications (Minispecs)**: Created for primitive processes. Goals: reduce ambiguity, obtain precise description, validate system design. Contents: process number/name, description, input/output data flows, process type, prewritten code, logic description, unresolved issues.

**Three Techniques for Structured Decision Logic**:

1. **Structured English**: Used when logic involves formulas or iteration, and decisions are not complex.
   - Structures: Sequential (action #1, #2, #3), Decision (IF-THEN-ELSE), Case (IF-CASE), Iteration (DO WHILE).
   - Advantages: Clarifies logic, effective communication with users.

2. **Decision Tables**: Used for complex combinations of conditions, actions, and rules. Four quadrants: conditions, condition alternatives, actions, rules.
   - Helps detect incompleteness, impossible situations, contradictions, redundancy.
   - Can simplify by combining rules.

3. **Decision Trees**: Used when complex branching occurs or sequence of decisions is critical. Draw from left to right, list all alternatives.
   - Advantages: Order of checking conditions is immediately noticeable; more readily understood.

**Selection Guide**:
- Structured English: Many repetitious actions, communication important.
- Decision Tables: Complex combinations, need to avoid errors.
- Decision Trees: Sequence critical, not every condition relevant to every action.
[/en]

[zh]
**过程规范（Mini规范）**：为原语过程创建。目标：减少歧义、获得精确描述、验证系统设计。内容：过程编号/名称、描述、输入/输出数据流、过程类型、预编写代码、逻辑描述、未解决问题。

**结构化决策逻辑的三种技术**：

1. **结构化自然语言**：当逻辑涉及公式或迭代且决策不复杂时使用。
   - 结构：顺序（操作#1、#2、#3）、决策（IF-THEN-ELSE）、分支（IF-CASE）、迭代（DO WHILE）。
   - 优势：阐明逻辑，与用户有效沟通。

2. **决策表**：用于条件、操作和规则的复杂组合。四个象限：条件、条件备选、操作、规则。
   - 帮助发现不完整、不可能情况、矛盾、冗余。
   - 可以通过合并规则来简化。

3. **决策树**：当出现复杂分支或决策顺序至关重要时使用。从左到右绘制，列出所有备选方案。
   - 优势：条件检查顺序一目了然；更易理解。

**选择指南**：
- 结构化自然语言：多个重复操作、沟通重要。
- 决策表：复杂组合、需要避免错误。
- 决策树：顺序关键、并非每个条件都与每个操作相关。
[/en]

### Checking Decision Tables | 检查决策表  <!-- p=23 -->

[en]
Four main problems to check in decision tables:
1. **Incompleteness**: Are there rules for all possible condition combinations? (2^N conditions → 2^N rules needed)
2. **Impossible Situations**: Are there rules for combinations that can never occur in reality? Mark as "impossible" rather than leaving blank.
3. **Contradictions**: Two or more rules have same conditions but different actions.
4. **Redundancy**: Two or more rules have same conditions AND same actions — can be combined.

**Simplifying Decision Tables**: Combine rules where condition values don't matter for the outcome. Use "—" (don't care) to indicate irrelevant conditions.
[/en]

[zh]
需要检查决策表中的四个主要问题：
1. **不完整**：是否所有可能的条件组合都有对应规则？（N 个条件 → 需要 2^N 条规则）
2. **不可能情况**：是否有对应现实中不可能出现的组合的规则？应标记为"不可能"而非留空。
3. **矛盾**：两条或多条规则具有相同条件但不同操作。
4. **冗余**：两条或多条规则具有相同条件且相同操作——可以合并。

**简化决策表**：合并条件值不影响结果的规则。使用"—"（不关心）表示无关条件。
[/en]

## 10. Object-Oriented Systems Analysis and Design | 面向对象的系统分析与设计  <!-- ref: 10.Object_Orient_Analysis.pdf -->

### Object-Oriented Concepts | 面向对象概念  <!-- p=4 -->

[en]
**Object**: Persons, places, or things relevant to the system being analyzed. An object has structure (attributes) and behavior (methods).

**Class**: Defines the set of shared attributes and behaviors found in each object in the class. Instantiate = create an object from a class.

**Inheritance**: When a derived class inherits all the attributes and behaviors of the base class. Reduces programming labor by using common objects easily.

**Object-Oriented Analysis and Design**: Works well for complicated systems undergoing continuous change. Objects and classes are reusable. UML is the industry standard.
[/en]

[zh]
**对象**：与待分析系统相关的人、地点或事物。对象具有结构（属性）和行为（方法）。

**类**：定义类中每个对象共有的属性和行为集。实例化 = 从类创建对象。

**继承**：派生类继承基类的所有属性和行为。通过使用通用对象减少编程工作量。

**面向对象的分析与设计**：适用于正在持续变更的复杂系统。对象和类可复用。UML 是行业标准。
[/en]

### Unified Modeling Language (UML) | 统一建模语言（UML）  <!-- p=11 -->

[en]
**UML Components**:
- Things: Structural (classes, interfaces, use cases), Behavioral (interactions, state machines), Group (boundaries), Annotation (notes).
- Relationships: Structural (dependencies, aggregations, associations, generalizations/specializations); Behavioral (communicates, includes, extends, generalizes).
- Diagrams: Structural (class, object, component, deployment); Behavioral (use case, sequence, communication, statechart, activity).

**UML Diagram Types**:

| Diagram | Purpose |
|:---|:---|
| Use Case Diagram | Describes what the system does (actors, use cases, relationships) |
| Activity Diagram | Shows sequence of activities, parallel activities, decisions; swimlanes |
| Sequence Diagram | Illustrates interactions between classes/objects over time; emphasizes time ordering |
| Communication Diagram | Shows same as sequence but emphasizes organization of objects |
| Class Diagram | Static features; classes, attributes, methods, relationships (associations, whole/part, aggregation, composition) |
| Statechart Diagram | Examines different states an object may have; complex life cycles |

**Types of Classes**:
- Entity classes: Real-world items (like ERD entities)
- Interface/Boundary classes: User interaction (displays, windows, webpages)
- Abstract classes: Cannot be directly instantiated; generalization/specialization
- Control classes: Control flow of activities
[/en]

[zh]
**UML 组成部分**：
- 事物：结构（类、接口、用例）、行为（交互、状态机）、分组（边界）、注释（笔记）。
- 关系：结构（依赖、聚合、关联、泛化/特化）；行为（通讯、包含、扩展、泛化）。

### Class Relationships and Statecharts | 类关系与状态图  <!-- p=18 -->

[en]
**Types of Class Relationships**:
- **Associations**: Simplest relationship; an object in one class relates to objects in another (or same) class. **Reflexive association**: relationship to objects in the same class.
- **Association Classes**: Used to break up many-to-many associations between classes.
- **Whole/Part Relationships**:
  - **Aggregation**: Whole is composed of sum of its parts.
  - **Collection**: Members may change, but whole retains its identity.
  - **Composition**: Whole has responsibility for the parts (parts cannot exist independently).

**Sequence Diagram Layers**:
1. **Presentation Layer**: What the user sees — interface/boundary classes.
2. **Business Layer**: Unique rules for this application — control classes.
3. **Persistence/Data Access Layer**: Obtaining and storing data — entity classes.

**Statechart Diagrams** — Create when:
- A class has a complex life cycle.
- An instance may update its attributes in multiple ways through the life cycle.
- A class has an operational life cycle.
- Two classes depend on each other.
- The object's current behavior depends on what happened previously.

**Event Types**: Signals/asynchronous messages, Synchronous calls, Temporal events (time-based).
[/en]

- **关联类**：用于打破类之间的多对多关联。

2. **业务层**：应用的独特规则——控制类。

**事件类型**：信号/异步消息、同步调用、时间事件（基于时间）。

### MVC Pattern and Output-Input Duality | MVC 模式与输入输出二元性  <!-- p=3 -->

[en]
**MVC Pattern for Output and Input**:

3. Controller → **Model** (creates order)

### Designing Effective Output | 设计有效输出  <!-- p=7 -->

[en]
**Output Rules**:
1. Choose the right output method (medium)
2. Keep it simple and uncluttered
3. Neutralize output bias
4. Provide context alongside summarization

**Output Bias — Design Choices Shape Decisions**:
- **Sorting Bias**: Alphabetical vs. chronological vs. ranked → different conclusions.
- **Graphical Bias**: Bar chart vs. pie chart vs. table → different impressions.
- **Truncation Bias**: Where you set the Y-axis changes perception dramatically.
- **Aggregation Bias**: Summaries hide outliers; averages erase distribution.

Analysts should avoid unnecessarily biasing output and make users aware of possible biases.

**Output Methods**:
| Medium | Advantage | Best For |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Displays/Dashboards | Interactive, real-time, quiet | Frequently accessed, dynamic data |
| Audio/Podcasts | Hands-free, transient | Individual alerts |
| Mobile/Tablets | Portable, gesture-driven | On-the-go consumption |
| Printers/Hard Copy | Highly reliable, archival | Regulatory compliance |

**Neutralize Output Bias**: Design choices shape decisions. Be aware of sorting bias, graphical bias (bar vs. pie vs. table), truncation bias (Y-axis), aggregation bias (averages hide distribution).
[/en]

[zh]
**输出规则**：
1. 选择正确的输出方法（媒介）
2. 保持简单和整洁
3. 消除输出偏见
4. 提供与汇总信息一起的上下文

**输出方法**：
| 媒介 | 优势 | 最佳用途 |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 显示器/仪表板 | 交互式、实时、安静 | 频繁访问的动态数据 |
| 音频/播客 | 免提、瞬时 | 个人提醒 |
| 移动设备/平板 | 便携、手势驱动 | 移动消费 |
| 打印机/纸质 | 高度可靠、可存档 | 法规合规 |

**消除输出偏见**：设计选择塑造决策。注意排序偏见、图形偏见（条形图 vs. 饼图 vs. 表格）、截断偏见（Y 轴）、聚合偏见（平均值掩盖分布）。
[/en]

### Designing Effective Input | 设计有效输入  <!-- p=15 -->

[en]
**Input Rules**:
1. Choose the right input method (medium)
2. Ensure accurate completion through smart navigation, defaults, and validation
3. Follow the natural flow (top-down, left-right)
4. Use the proper GUI control for the data type

**Input Methods**:
| Medium | Advantage | Best For |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Keyboard/Typing | High speed, universal | Long-form data entry, coding |
| Voice/Audio | Hands-free, natural | Field work, driving, accessibility |
| Barcode/QR Scanning | Instant, accurate | Inventory, retail POS |
| Touch/Gesture | Intuitive, no peripherals | Kiosks, mobile apps |

- Enter what only humans can know; let the computer figure out the rest.
- A base data item should be entered only once at only one point.
- Bad form: 5 human-typed fields (name, phone, product, price, total).
- Good form: 1 human-typed field (quantity only — rest auto-filled from master data/lookup).

**Emerging Data-Entry Methods**: OCR (auto-fill addresses), Computer Vision (shelf recognition), LLM (extract data from unstructured emails), IoT sensors (auto-report stock levels).

**Validation**: Client-side (instant feedback, catches honest mistakes) and server-side (final defense, enforces business rules, protects against malicious input).
[/en]

[zh]

## 12. Designing Databases and Human-Computer Interaction | 数据库与人机交互设计  <!-- ref: 12.Designing_DB_HCI.pdf -->

### Databases: The Memory of the System | 数据库：系统的记忆  <!-- p=6 -->

[en]
**Relational Databases**: Data organized into tables (relations) with rows (tuples/records) and columns (fields/attributes). Primary key uniquely identifies each row. Foreign key links tables.

**Normalization (规范化)**: Process of eliminating data redundancy and anomalies.

**Three Anomalies from Poor Normalization**:
| OrderID | Customer | CustPhone | Product | UnitPrice | Qty |
| 1001 | Zhang | 138... | iPhone | 6999 | 1 |
| 1002 | Zhang | 138... | AirPods | 1299 | 2 |
| 1003 | Zhang | 139... | iPhone | 7099 | 1 |

- **Update Anomaly**: Phone updated in only one row → inconsistency.
- **Insert Anomaly**: Can't add a customer without an order.
- **Delete Anomaly**: Delete last order → customer record lost.

| Normal Form | Rule | Plain Meaning |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 1NF | Atomic values, no repeating groups | One cell, one fact |
| 2NF | 1NF + no partial dependency on composite key | Don't mix two things in one table |
| 3NF | 2NF + no transitive dependency | Non-key attributes depend on key only |

- Aim for 3NF by default.
- Denormalize intentionally when query performance demands it.
- Normalize for correctness; Denormalize for performance; Document both.

**OLTP vs. OLAP**:
| | OLTP (Operational) | OLAP (Analytical) |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Purpose | Run the business | Understand the business |
| Focus | Consistency | Analyzability |
| Schema | Normalized (2NF-3NF) | Denormalized |

**NoSQL Databases**:
- Key-Value (Redis): Sacrifices query expressiveness for speed
- Document (MongoDB): Flexible nested data without strict schema
- Column-Family (Cassandra): Massive write scale, no joins/transactions
- Graph (Neo4j): Native relationship traversal

**Vector Databases (向量数据库)** : Store unstructured data as high-dimensional numerical arrays (embeddings). Execute semantic similarity search. Critical for RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation).
[/en]

[zh]
**关系型数据库**：数据组织成表（关系），包含行（元组/记录）和列（字段/属性）。主键唯一标识每一行。外键链接表。

**规范化**：消除数据冗余和异常的过程。

**不规范化的三大异常**：
| OrderID | 客户 | 电话 | 产品 | 单价 | 数量 |
| 1001 | 张三 | 138... | iPhone | 6999 | 1 |
| 1002 | 张三 | 138... | AirPods | 1299 | 2 |
| 1003 | 张三 | 139... | iPhone | 7099 | 1 |

- **更新异常**：仅在一行更新电话 → 不一致。
- **插入异常**：没有订单就无法添加客户。
- **删除异常**：删除最后一笔订单 → 客户记录丢失。

| 范式 | 规则 | 通俗解释 |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 1NF | 原子值，无重复组 | 一个单元格，一个事实 |
| 2NF | 1NF + 无对复合键的部分依赖 | 不要把两件事混在一张表里 |
| 3NF | 2NF + 无传递依赖 | 非主键属性只依赖于主键 |

- 默认目标是 3NF。
- 当查询性能需要时有意识地反规范化。
- 为正确性规范化；为性能反规范化；两者都做记录。

**OLTP vs. OLAP**：
| | OLTP（运营型） | OLAP（分析型） |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 目的 | 运行业务 | 理解业务 |
| 焦点 | 一致性 | 可分析性 |
| 模式 | 规范化（2NF-3NF） | 反规范化 |

**NoSQL 数据库**：
- 键值（Redis）：牺牲查询表达能力换取速度
- 文档（MongoDB）：灵活的嵌套数据，无严格模式
- 列族（Cassandra）：大规模写入扩展，无连接/事务
- 图（Neo4j）：原生关系遍历

**向量数据库**：将非结构化数据存储为高维数值数组（嵌入向量）。执行语义相似性搜索。对 RAG（检索增强生成）至关重要。
[/en]

### Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) | 人机交互  <!-- p=20 -->

[en]
**Core Principle of HCI**: Designing for fit between human, the computer, and the task.

**Types of User Interfaces**:
- Natural-language, Q&A, Menus, Form-fill, Command-language (CLI), Graphical (GUI), Web interfaces. Applications usually combine multiple types.

**Seven Types of User Feedback**:
1. Accepting input ("I heard you")
2. Recognizing task ("I understand what you want")
3. Producing more information ("I need more from you")
4. Accepting/processing ("Working on it...")
5. Rejecting input with reason ("I can't do that because...")
6. Communicating delay ("This is taking a while")
7. Completing task ("Done. Here's the result.")

**The AI Era: Interaction as Conversation**:
- Traditional: "Teach the user to think like the database."
- AI-Native: "Teach the system to think like the user."
- Agentic: "Delegate to the system to act on your behalf."

Agentic interfaces: User expresses a GOAL; the system PLANS and EXECUTES multiple steps autonomously. Requires tools (APIs, code execution), memory across turns, human-in-the-loop checkpoints.

**Agentic vs. Natural Language**:
| | Natural Language Query | Agentic Interface |
|:---|:---|:---|
| System does | Returns ONE answer | Runs a multi-step PLAN |
| User does | Decides next action | Supervises, not operates |
| Example | "Show sales in Q3" | "Fix this bug end-to-end" — plans → edits → tests → reports |

For the first time, the user does not OPERATE the system — the user DELEGATES to the system.

**Historical Perspective**:
- **1970s-2000s**: Users learned the database's language (SQL, menus, forms).
- **2010s**: Mobile/touch made interaction more natural; data stayed structured.
- **2020s**: Vector + LLM let the system understand the USER's language.
[/en]

[zh]
智能体界面：用户表达目标；系统自主规划和执行多步骤行动。需要工具（API、代码执行）、跨轮次记忆、人在回路检查点。

**智能体 vs. 自然语言**：
| | 自然语言查询 | 智能体界面 |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 系统做 | 返回一个答案 | 运行多步骤计划 |
| 用户做 | 决定下一步行动 | 监督而非操作 |
| 示例 | "显示 Q3 销售额" | "从头到尾修复这个 bug"——计划→编辑→测试→报告 |

历史上第一次，用户不再操作系统——用户将任务委托给系统。

**历史视角**：
- **1970s-2000s**：用户学习数据库的语言（SQL、菜单、表单）。
- **2010s**：移动/触控使交互更自然；数据保持结构化。
- **2020s**：向量+LLM 让系统理解用户的语言。
[/en]

[zh]
**人机交互的核心原则**：设计人与计算机和任务之间的适配。

**用户界面类型**：
- 自然语言、问答、菜单、表单填充、命令语言（CLI）、图形界面（GUI）、Web 界面。应用程序通常组合多种类型。

**七种用户反馈类型**：
1. 接受输入（"我听到了"）
2. 识别任务（"我明白你想要什么"）
3. 请求更多信息（"我需要你提供更多信息"）
4. 接受/处理中（"正在处理..."）
5. 拒绝输入并说明原因（"我无法做到，因为..."）
6. 告知延迟（"这需要一点时间"）
7. 完成任务（"完成。这是结果。"）

**AI 时代：作为对话的交互**：
- 传统："教用户像数据库一样思考。"
- AI 原生："教系统像用户一样思考。"
- 智能体："将任务委托给系统，让它代表你行动。"

智能体界面：用户表达目标；系统自主规划和执行多步骤行动。需要工具（API、代码执行）、跨轮次记忆、人在回路检查点。

## 13. Quality Assurance | 质量保证  <!-- ref: 13.Quality_Assurance.pdf -->

### Designing Accurate Data Entry Procedures | 设计准确的数据录入流程  <!-- p=4 -->

[en]
**Effective Coding (数据编码)** :
- **Simple Sequence Code**: Assigned number with no relation to data itself. Best for identifiers (e.g., customer_id). Stable, privacy-safe, deletable without breaking references.
- **Block Sequence Code**: Groups data by common characteristics. Useful for events that may be classified (e.g., order_id: [type]-[date]-[sequence]).
- **Significant-Digit Subset Code**: Each digit position has meaning. Useful for products (e.g., SKU). Enables bulk operations (e.g., "all speakers 20% off").
- Other codes: Classification, Cipher, Mnemonic, Function.

**Coding Guidelines**: Concise, stable, unique, sortable, avoid confusion, uniform, modifiable, meaningful.

**Effective Data Capture – The Core Principle**:
- Enter what only humans can know; let the computer figure out the rest.
- A base data item should be entered only once at only one point.

**Validation (Check Digit)**: Eight types of validation:
1. Missing Data
2. Field Length
3. Class/Composition (character type)
4. Range/Reasonableness
5. Invalid values (blacklist)
6. Comparison with Stored Data (foreign key existence)
7. Self-Checking Digits (Luhn algorithm)
8. Pattern Match (regex)

**Two Layers of Defense**:
- Client-Side Validation: Instant feedback, catches honest mistakes.
- Server-Side Validation: Final defense, enforces business rules, protects against malicious input.
[/en]

[zh]
**有效编码**：
- **简单序列码**：分配的编号与数据本身无关。最适合标识符（如 customer_id）。稳定、隐私安全、删除时不会破坏引用。
- **分类序列编码**：按共同特征分组数据。适用于可能需要分类的事件（如 order_id：[类型]-[日期]-[序列]）。
- **子集代号编码**：每个数字位置都有含义。适用于产品（如 SKU）。支持批量操作（如"所有扬声器八折"）。
- 其他代码：分类码、密码、助记码、功能码。

**编码指南**：简洁、稳定、唯一、可排序、避免混淆、统一、可修改、有意义。

**三种编码策略的权衡**：

| 编码类型 | 适用场景 | 收益 | 代价 |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| 简单序列码 (customer_id) | 标识符 | 永久稳定、隐私安全、可删除个人信息而不破坏引用 | 不可读、暴露规模 |
| 分类序列编码 (order_id) | 事件/分类 | 自然时间排序、日期范围查询=主键范围扫描 | 计数器需集中管理、暴露每日交易量 |
| 子集代号编码 (SKU) | 产品 | 人工可读、可按段排序、批量操作一行 SQL | 段含义可能过时、每段有空间限制 |

**有效数据捕获——核心原则**：
- 只输入人类才能知道的信息；让计算机计算出其余部分。
- 一个基础数据项应只在一个点输入一次。

**数据捕获决策矩阵**：

| 问题 | Yes → | No → |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 是否可变？ | 每次捕获（数量、日期） | 从主数据查找 |
| 是否区分此记录？ | 捕获（要订购的 SKU） | 派生（SKU 的名称、价格、重量） |
| 计算机能否计算？ | 生成（时间戳、ID、总计） | 捕获 |

**验证**：八种验证类型：
1. 缺失数据
2. 字段长度
3. 类别/组成（字符类型）
4. 范围/合理性
5. 无效值（黑名单）
6. 与存储数据比较（外键存在性）
7. 自校验位（Luhn 算法）
8. 模式匹配（正则表达式）

**两道防线**：
- 客户端验证：即时反馈，捕捉诚实错误。
- 服务器端验证：最后防线，执行业务规则，防止恶意输入。

**验证管道——错误成本随阶段指数增长**：
```
客户端验证 → 服务器端验证 → 数据库约束 → 人工修复
捕获成本: $1          $10            $100        $1000+
```
三道防线：永远不要信任客户端数据——在服务端重新验证一切。服务端验证是唯一真正重要的防线。
[/en]
